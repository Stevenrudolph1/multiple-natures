## Quick Reference: The Seven Cardinal Rules

These rules are inviolable. Memorize them.

1. **Never type people.** Natures describe demands, not identities.

2. **Never prescribe.** What someone does with MN information is theirs.

3. **Never promise transformation.** MN provides orientation, not change.

4. **Never predict.** MN observes patterns; it doesn't forecast outcomes.

5. **Never use Gardner's terms.** Gross/Fine Bodily, Graphic/Spatial Visual, Logical.

6. **Never claim completeness.** MN addresses one dimension, not all of life.

7. **Always return agency.** The choice belongs to the person, not the framework.

## Quick Reference: Voice Calibration

**MN sounds like:**
- A clear-thinking colleague after a long meeting
- Someone who has seen this pattern many times
- A practical philosopher who cares about getting things right

**MN does NOT sound like:**
- A salesperson promising transformation
- A therapist offering emotional support
- A guru with hidden wisdom
- A motivational speaker building urgency

**Sentence rhythm:**
- Short sentences carry weight
- Space between ideas
- Stories precede concepts
- Return to key phrases

## Quick Reference: The Core Distinction

This is the foundation. Every piece of content should reflect it:

**MI (Multiple Intelligences):**
- Describes capacity
- The channel through which work moves
- Answers: "How CAN this person work?"

**MN (Multiple Natures):**
- Describes cost
- The source of energy
- Answers: "What does it COST to keep working this way?"

**Together:**
- MI without MN turns ability into obligation
- MN restores the question of sustainability
- Both are needed; neither is complete alone

## Maintenance Notes

This canon should be treated as a living document set. Updates should be made when:

1. **New philosophical understanding emerges** — If MN's understanding of its own limits evolves, the canon must reflect this.

2. **Drift patterns are identified** — If AI consistently drifts in new ways, add those patterns to Document 07.

3. **New content types are needed** — If new page types emerge, add specifications to Document 06.

4. **Terminology evolves** — If language choices are refined, update Documents 02 and 03.

All updates should be tracked in version history and reviewed against the foundational philosophy in Document 01.

## The Standard

Every piece of content should leave the reader:
- More accurately oriented
- Less burdened by false explanation
- With their agency intact

That is the measure of success.

If content achieves this, it is canon-compliant.

If it does not, it requires revision.

The restraint is the competence.
