# Page-by-Page Content Specifications

**For AI-Assisted Website Content Generation**

---

## Purpose

This document provides specific structural and content requirements for each major page type on multiplenatures.com. Use these specifications as constraints when generating content.

---

## Homepage

### Purpose
Introduce visitors to MN and orient them to what they'll find on the site.

### Required Elements

**Hero Section**
- Lead with recognition, not promise
- Avoid urgency
- Do not promise transformation

**Opening hook options (choose one pattern):**
- Problem recognition: "You've felt this: [situation where standard explanations don't fit]"
- Question pattern: "What if the explanation isn't about you at all?"
- Statement: "MN names something you may already recognize."

**NOT acceptable:**
- "Discover who you really are"
- "Transform your life"
- "Unlock your potential"

**The Core Explanation**
Must include:
- What situations demand / what people supply / what it costs
- The MI + MN distinction (channel vs. source, capacity vs. sustainability)
- Clear statement that MN provides orientation, not direction

**What MN Is Not Section**
Must explicitly state:
- Not a personality test
- Not a typing system
- Not predictive
- Not prescriptive

**Call to Action**
- "Take the MNTEST" or "Explore the framework"
- No urgency language ("Don't wait!", "Limited time!")
- Permission-granting tone

### Tone
Measured, warm, clear. Like a colleague explaining something they've noticed for years.

### Length
400-600 words maximum for main content (not including navigation elements)

## MNTEST Page

### Purpose
Explain what the MNTEST is and isn't, and invite visitors to take it.

### Required Sections

**What the MNTEST Does**
- Surfaces patterns in engagement
- Offers starting point for observation
- Highlights what flows easily vs. what costs
- Provides language for what you may already recognize

**What the MNTEST Does NOT Do**
Must explicitly state:
- Does not diagnose
- Does not predict outcomes
- Does not define identity
- Does not tell you what to do
- Is not a psychometric instrument

**What to Expect**
- Approximate time to complete
- Format/structure
- What results will look like
- How to interpret (starting point, not conclusion)

**Call to Action**
- Clear button to begin
- No urgency
- Optional: "Take it when you're ready"

### Prohibited Content
- "Discover your true self"
- "Find your purpose"
- Career matching promises
- Transformation claims
- Urgency tactics

### Tone
Inviting but not pushing. Confidence without overselling.

### Length
300-500 words

## About Page (Steven Rudolph / Multiple Natures Story)

### Purpose
Share the origin story in a way that grounds MN in practical observation, not theory.

### Required Narrative Elements

**The Origin**
- Must mention: teaching in New York, then Japan
- Must include: noticing patterns that MI didn't explain
- Must mention: building schools in India
- Must include: expansion to adults in France

**The Evolution**
Must acknowledge:
- Early versions were used more freely (including prescription)
- Over time, limits became clearer
- The current understanding includes what MN DOESN'T do
- This correction is part of the story, not hidden

**The Current Position**
- MN provides orientation, not direction
- The limits are the design, not a flaw
- What comes after recognition belongs to the person

### Tone
- Honest about the journey including corrections
- Warm but not self-aggrandizing
- Grounded in practical observation, not theoretical brilliance

### Prohibited Content
- Hagiographic origin story
- "Revolutionary discovery"
- Claims of being first or only
- Promises about what MN will become

### Length
500-800 words

## Nine Natures Overview Page

### Purpose
Introduce the Nine Natures framework without creating types.

### Required Framing
Opening must establish:
- Natures describe what situations demand, not who people are
- Everyone can supply all nine
- The only question is cost

### For Each Nature
Include:
- Name
- Brief description (1-2 sentences) of the engagement it describes
- What situations pull for it
- What it looks like when supplied easily
- What it looks like when costly

Do NOT include:
- "People who are [Nature]..."
- Career recommendations
- "If you're high in [Nature]..."
- Type descriptions

### Closing
- Reiterate that these are situational, not personal
- Note that patterns can be explored through the MNTEST
- Return agency: what you do with the information is yours

### Length
800-1200 words total (80-120 per Nature plus framing)

## Individual Nature Pages (9 pages)

### Purpose
Deep dive into a single Nature without creating type identity.

### Required Sections

**Opening Definition**
- What engagement this Nature describes
- What situations demand it
- Frame as situational, not identity

**What Situations Pull for [Nature]**
- 5-8 concrete examples of contexts
- Workplace, personal, relationship, educational examples

**When [Nature] Engagement Flows Easily**
- Description of what it looks and feels like
- Energy, ease, sustainability
- No trait language ("Protectives are vigilant")

**When [Nature] Engagement Costs**
- What happens when this isn't someone's easy supply
- Fatigue, effort, recovery needs
- No deficiency language

**Common Misreadings**
- How this Nature gets misunderstood
- Labels that often get applied
- Why those miss the situational frame

**What MN Doesn't Claim**
- Does not predict outcomes
- Does not prescribe careers
- Does not define identity

### Prohibited Content
- "[Nature] people should..."
- "If you're a [Nature]..."
- Career recommendations
- Relationship prescriptions

### Tone
Observational, specific, grounded in examples.

### Length
600-900 words

## Multiple Intelligences Page

### Purpose
Explain the MI framework as MN uses it, with modifications clearly stated.

### Required Content

**The Relationship**
- MI = capacity, channel, how
- MN = cost, source, sustainability
- MI without MN collapses ability into obligation

**The Modifications**
Must explicitly state:
- Gardner's Bodily-Kinesthetic split into Gross Bodily + Fine Bodily
- Gardner's Spatial split into Graphic Visual + Spatial Visual
- This gives 10, not Gardner's 8
- Reason for modification (practical observation)

**The Ten Intelligences**
Brief description of each:
1. Gross Bodily
2. Fine Bodily
3. Interpersonal
4. Intrapersonal
5. Logical
6. Linguistic
7. Graphic Visual
8. Spatial Visual
9. Musical
10. Naturalistic

**What MI Doesn't Tell You**
- Cannot tell you what feeds vs. drains
- Cannot predict sustainability
- Cannot determine where you should work

### Prohibited Content
- Using Gardner's original terms (bodily-kinesthetic, visual-spatial)
- "Visual learners should..."
- Learning style prescriptions

### Length
500-700 words

## Certification/Training Page

### Purpose
Describe practitioner training without overclaiming outcomes.

### Required Content

**What Certification Involves**
- Learning the framework accurately
- Using language precisely
- Recognizing drift and overclaim
- Maintaining limits in client work
- Practice in observation without prescription

**What Certification Is NOT**
- Not license to diagnose
- Not permission to prescribe
- Not authority to direct lives
- Not a complete method

**Who It's For**
- Coaches, educators, counselors, HR professionals
- Those who want to add MN as a lens
- Those willing to work within limits

**What Certified Practitioners Do**
- Help others see patterns
- Provide language for what clients recognize
- Maintain boundaries
- Return agency to clients

### Prohibited Content
- "Help people find their purpose"
- "Transform clients' lives"
- Transformation claims
- Urgency tactics

### Tone
Professional, clear about scope, honest about limits.

### Length
400-600 words

## Blog Post Template

### Purpose
Provide educational content that maintains canon.

### Required Structure

**Opening**
- Start with observation or situation, not theory
- Ground in recognizable human moment
- Avoid "In this post, I'll show you..."

**Body**
- Use concrete examples before abstract concepts
- Maintain situational framing throughout
- Include what MN doesn't claim
- No prescriptive conclusions

**Closing**
- Do not tell reader what to do
- Return agency
- Avoid transformation promises
- Permitted: "If this resonates, you might explore..."

### Prohibited Patterns
- "5 ways to use your Nature..."
- "If you're [Nature], try..."
- "How to leverage your Intelligences"
- Listicles with prescriptions
- Transformation narratives

### Length
600-1200 words

## Social Media

### Post Patterns (Acceptable)

**Observation pattern:**
"Situations demand. People supply. The cost appears later—sometimes as fatigue after success, sometimes as resistance before starting. MN helps you see the difference."

**Question pattern:**
"When did you last feel depleted by something you did well?"

**Definition pattern:**
"Orientation: knowing where you are. Direction: knowing where to go. MN provides the first. The second is yours."

### Post Patterns (Prohibited)

- "Find your purpose with MN!"
- "Which Nature are you? Take the quiz!"
- "Discover your true self"
- "Transform your career today"
- Engagement bait with types

### Character Limits
- Twitter: 280 characters
- Instagram: 125 characters before "more"
- LinkedIn: 150 characters before "see more"

Keep the most important content above fold.

## Universal Rules for All Pages

1. **Never create urgency** — No "Don't wait," "Limited time," "Act now"

2. **Never promise transformation** — No "Change your life," "Find your purpose," "Unlock potential"

3. **Never prescribe** — No "You should," "If you're [Nature], do..."

4. **Never type** — No "[Nature]s are...," "As a [Nature]..."

5. **Always include limits** — Every substantive page should state what MN doesn't do

6. **Always return agency** — End with choice belonging to the reader

7. **Always use correct terminology** — Never Gardner's original terms

8. **Always maintain situational frame** — Natures describe demands, not people
