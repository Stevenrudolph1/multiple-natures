# FAQ Canon

**Exact Phrasing for Common Questions**

---

## Purpose

This document provides canon-compliant answers to the most common questions about Multiple Natures. Use these exact phrasings or close variations to prevent drift.

Each entry includes:
- The question as commonly asked
- The canon-compliant answer
- What to avoid in the answer

---

## About Multiple Natures

### Q: What is Multiple Natures?

**Canon Answer:**
Multiple Natures is an observational framework that names what situations demand and what people supply in response—and what it costs when those don't match.

It sits alongside Multiple Intelligences:
- MI describes how people can work (channels of capacity)
- MN describes what it costs them to keep working that way (sources of energy)

MN provides orientation—seeing where you are. It does not provide direction—telling you where to go.

**Avoid:**
- "A personality assessment"
- "A way to discover who you really are"
- "A system for finding your purpose"

---

### Q: What is Multiple Natures NOT?

**Canon Answer:**
MN is not:
- A personality theory or typing system
- A diagnostic framework
- A predictive model
- A strengths assessment
- Therapy or counseling
- A motivation model
- Identity capture

MN deliberately stops before these things—because going further would make it inaccurate.

**Avoid:**
- Hedging on these limits
- "It's kind of like a personality test but better"

---

### Q: How is MN different from personality tests like Myers-Briggs or DISC?

**Canon Answer:**
Personality tests describe who you are—they assign types, traits, or categories.

MN describes something different: what situations demand and what it costs you to meet those demands.

MN doesn't tell you who you are. It shows you what shows up repeatedly when you meet different contexts. The frame stays situational, not personal.

**Avoid:**
- "It's more accurate than other tests"
- "It gives you your true type"
- Claiming superiority over other frameworks

---

### Q: Where did Multiple Natures come from?

**Canon Answer:**
MN emerged from practical contexts over decades—classrooms, teams, organizations, hiring, families.

Steven Rudolph developed it through direct observation: teaching in New York and Japan, building and running schools in India, and working with adults across Europe. It wasn't invented theoretically; it was noticed repeatedly and given language.

The framework has also evolved. Early on, it was used more freely—including for decisions and prescriptions. Over time, the limits became clearer: MN provides orientation, not direction. That correction is part of its development.

**Avoid:**
- "Discovered the truth about human nature"
- "Revolutionary breakthrough"
- Hagiographic origin story

## About the MNTEST

### Q: What is the MNTEST?

**Canon Answer:**
The MNTEST is an assessment that surfaces patterns in how you engage with different situations.

It highlights tendencies—recurring modes of engagement that appear when you meet various demands. It offers a starting point for observation and conversation.

The MNTEST does not diagnose, predict outcomes, define identity, or tell you what to do.

**Avoid:**
- "Tells you who you are"
- "Reveals your true nature"
- "Scientifically validated instrument"

---

### Q: What will the MNTEST tell me?

**Canon Answer:**
The MNTEST will show you:
- Which modes of engagement tend to flow easily for you
- Which modes may carry more cost when sustained
- Patterns you may already recognize but haven't had language for

It will not tell you:
- What career to pursue
- Who you should be with
- What decisions to make
- What your life should look like

**Avoid:**
- Promising revelation or transformation
- "Everything you need to know about yourself"

---

### Q: Is the MNTEST scientifically validated?

**Canon Answer:**
The MNTEST is not a psychometric instrument in the clinical sense. It does not claim the kind of validation that standardized psychological assessments undergo.

What it does is surface patterns that people consistently recognize as accurate to their experience. Its value is practical—it starts useful conversations—not statistical.

**Avoid:**
- False claims of validation
- Defensive dismissal of the question
- "Better than validated tests"

---

### Q: Can the MNTEST tell me what career I should pursue?

**Canon Answer:**
No. The MNTEST shows what different engagements cost you—not what you should do with that information.

If certain engagement patterns flow easily for you, work that pulls for them may feel more sustainable. But career choice involves many factors MN doesn't address: economics, opportunity, values, relationships, timing.

MN can inform career thinking. It cannot decide it.

**Avoid:**
- "Yes, it will point you toward your ideal career"
- Career matching or prescription

## About the Nine Natures

### Q: What are the Nine Natures?

**Canon Answer:**
The Nine Natures describe recurring modes of engagement that situations pull for:

1. **Protective** — vigilance, accuracy, error prevention
2. **Educative** — explaining, clarifying, teaching
3. **Administrative** — organization, follow-through, maintenance
4. **Creative** — idea generation, novelty, new combinations
5. **Healing** — care, repair, presence with difficulty
6. **Entertaining** — energy, performance, atmosphere-shaping
7. **Providing** — service, support, meeting needs
8. **Entrepreneurial** — initiative, ownership, building
9. **Adventurous** — risk, novelty, uncertainty

Everyone can supply all nine. The question is what it costs to supply each one over time.

**Avoid:**
- Framing as personality types
- "You are your dominant Nature"

---

### Q: Am I just one Nature?

**Canon Answer:**
No. You supply all nine Natures—the question is relative cost.

Some modes of engagement flow easily; you meet those demands without strain. Others you can supply, but it takes more from you. The pattern across all nine describes something real, but it doesn't reduce you to a type.

**Avoid:**
- "Your primary Nature is..."
- Ranking or hierarchy that implies identity

---

### Q: Can my Natures change?

**Canon Answer:**
MN describes patterns that tend to persist over time—but MN also doesn't claim permanence.

What shows up repeatedly across contexts tends to keep showing up. But MN stays observational: it names what appears, not what must always appear.

The framework is agnostic about whether patterns can change. It focuses on what is present now, not what is fixed forever.

**Avoid:**
- "Your Natures are fixed"
- "You can change your Natures"
- Claiming certainty either direction

## About Multiple Intelligences

### Q: What is the relationship between Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures?

**Canon Answer:**
MI and MN work together but address different questions:

- **MI (Multiple Intelligences)** describes capacity—the channels through which learning, work, and expression move. How you CAN work.

- **MN (Multiple Natures)** describes cost—what it takes to keep working that way. What it COSTS to sustain.

MI without MN turns ability into obligation. If you can do something, you should keep doing it.

MN adds the missing question: is this engagement feeding you or depleting you?

**Avoid:**
- "MN is better than MI"
- Collapsing them together
- Ignoring MI entirely

---

### Q: How is your version of Multiple Intelligences different from Gardner's?

**Canon Answer:**
MN uses a modified version of Gardner's framework with two key differences:

1. Gardner's "Bodily-Kinesthetic Intelligence" is split into:
   - **Gross Bodily Intelligence** (whole-body movement)
   - **Fine Bodily Intelligence** (hand/precision work)

2. Gardner's "Spatial Intelligence" is split into:
   - **Graphic Visual Intelligence** (2D images, visual representation)
   - **Spatial Visual Intelligence** (3D space, navigation)

This gives us 10 Intelligences instead of Gardner's 8. The split reflects practical observation: whole-body coordination and fine motor precision are different channels.

**Avoid:**
- Using Gardner's original terms
- Claiming Gardner was "wrong"

## About Practical Application

### Q: What should I do with my MNTEST results?

**Canon Answer:**
That's yours to decide.

MN shows you where you are—what situations demand and what it costs you to meet them. What you do with that clarity is not something MN prescribes.

Some people use it to understand why certain situations feel harder than they should. Some use it to inform decisions about work or relationships. Some simply appreciate having language for what they already knew.

None of these is the "right" use.

**Avoid:**
- "You should..."
- Prescriptive guidance
- Implying there's a correct action

---

### Q: Can MN help me choose a career?

**Canon Answer:**
MN can inform career thinking by showing which engagement patterns flow easily for you and which carry cost.

But MN cannot choose a career for you. Career decisions involve economics, opportunity, values, relationships, timing, and many factors beyond what MN addresses.

Knowing what costs you less is useful information. It's not a decision.

**Avoid:**
- "Yes, MN will show you your ideal career"
- Career matching claims

---

### Q: Can MN help my relationship?

**Canon Answer:**
MN can give you language for patterns that may be creating friction—different engagement costs that show up as conflict.

But MN cannot fix relationships. Seeing patterns more clearly may help you talk about differences without blame. What you do with that clarity—whether and how you adjust—is relationship work, not framework work.

**Avoid:**
- "MN will transform your relationship"
- Promising outcomes

---

### Q: Can MN help me understand my child?

**Canon Answer:**
MN can help you see what different situations ask of your child and what those demands may cost.

It may clarify why some activities leave your child energized and others depleted—even when they perform well in both.

But MN doesn't define who your child is or predict their future. It offers observation, not conclusion.

**Avoid:**
- "Discover your child's true nature"
- Labeling children
- Predicting outcomes

## About Certification/Professional Use

### Q: What does MN practitioner certification involve?

**Canon Answer:**
MN practitioner certification trains you to work with the framework accurately—including knowing where it stops.

The curriculum includes:
- Understanding the theoretical foundations
- Using the language precisely
- Recognizing drift and overclaim
- Maintaining boundaries in client work
- Practicing observation without prescription

Certification is not permission to diagnose, predict, or prescribe. It's training in accurate orientation.

**Avoid:**
- "Learn to help people find their purpose"
- Transformation claims
- Implying certification grants authority to direct lives

---

### Q: Can I use MN in my coaching/therapy/counseling practice?

**Canon Answer:**
MN can be integrated into various professional contexts as a framework for observation and language.

It does not replace professional assessment, diagnosis, or treatment. It adds a particular lens for seeing what situations demand and what they cost—complementing, not replacing, your existing methods.

Professional ethical guidelines still apply. MN adds vocabulary; it doesn't change scope of practice.

**Avoid:**
- "MN can replace your current methods"
- Suggesting MN is sufficient on its own

## About Limits

### Q: What can't MN do?

**Canon Answer:**
MN cannot:
- Diagnose disorders or pathology
- Predict success, failure, or outcomes
- Tell you what to do
- Sort people for placement or selection
- Transform your life
- Replace professional help
- Provide complete self-knowledge

MN provides orientation in one domain: what situations demand and what it costs to meet them. That's all. What you do with that orientation is yours.

**Avoid:**
- Hedging on these limits
- "Well, it kind of can..."

---

### Q: Why doesn't MN tell me what to do?

**Canon Answer:**
Because telling you what to do would make MN inaccurate.

MN can see patterns. It cannot see your full situation—your constraints, opportunities, values, relationships, timing. Prescribing action would require knowing things MN doesn't know.

Orientation is complete when you see more clearly. Direction requires judgment MN cannot provide.

**Avoid:**
- Apologizing for the limit
- Implying it's a temporary limitation that will be fixed

---

### Q: Is MN enough to make major life decisions?

**Canon Answer:**
No.

MN addresses one dimension: what situations demand and what it costs you to meet them. Major life decisions involve many factors MN doesn't address.

MN can inform decisions by clarifying one relevant input. It cannot make decisions by itself—and should not be treated as if it can.

**Avoid:**
- "Yes, with MN you have what you need"
- Overstating MN's scope

---

## The Meta-Question

### Q: Why all the limits? Why doesn't MN do more?

**Canon Answer:**
Because the limits are what make everything before them accurate.

MN works precisely because it knows where it stops. Frameworks that claim to do more—predict outcomes, determine careers, transform lives—cannot maintain accuracy.

The restraint is not a weakness. It's what allows MN to describe something real rather than promising something fictional.

**Avoid:**
- Apologetic tone
- Implying MN should do more
- "We're working on expanding..."

## Response Template

When answering questions not covered here, follow this structure:

1. **Acknowledge the question** — Don't dismiss or redirect
2. **Answer what MN CAN address** — Stay in domain
3. **Name what MN CANNOT address** — State limits clearly
4. **Return agency** — "What you do with this is yours"

Example template:

"MN can show you [what's in scope]. It cannot [what's out of scope]. What you do with that clarity—[return agency phrase]—is not something MN decides for you."
