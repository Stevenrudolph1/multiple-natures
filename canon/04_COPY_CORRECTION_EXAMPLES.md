# Copy Correction Examples

**Before/After Examples for AI-Assisted Content Generation**

---

## Purpose

This document provides concrete examples of drifted copy corrected to canon-compliant copy. Use these as training examples to calibrate AI output.

Each example shows:
- **DRIFTED** — What AI commonly generates (incorrect)
- **PROBLEM** — Why it violates canon
- **CORRECTED** — Canon-compliant alternative

---

## Category 1: Identity/Typing Drift

### Example 1.1
**DRIFTED:**
"If you're a Creative, you'll thrive in environments that value innovation and new ideas."

**PROBLEM:**
- Creates identity category ("a Creative")
- Prescribes outcomes ("you'll thrive")
- Makes predictive claim

**CORRECTED:**
"Environments that pull for innovation and new ideas may feel sustaining if you supply Creative engagement easily. They may feel costly if generating novelty takes more from you than it gives."

---

### Example 1.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Protectives make excellent quality assurance professionals because they naturally spot errors."

**PROBLEM:**
- Creates category of people ("Protectives")
- Makes career prescription
- Claims "natural" ability as identity

**CORRECTED:**
"Quality assurance work pulls consistently for Protective engagement—vigilance, error detection, boundary-holding. Someone who supplies this engagement easily may find the work sustaining. Someone who can do it but finds it costly may succeed in the role while quietly depleting."

---

### Example 1.3
**DRIFTED:**
"As an Adventurous person, you're drawn to risk and excitement."

**PROBLEM:**
- Creates identity ("Adventurous person")
- Uses "you're drawn to" as trait

**CORRECTED:**
"If Adventurous engagement flows easily for you—if uncertainty energizes rather than destabilizes—situations that pull for risk may feel enlivening. This describes what a context asks for and what it costs you to meet that demand."

---

### Example 1.4
**DRIFTED:**
"Healers are naturally empathetic and caring individuals."

**PROBLEM:**
- Creates type ("Healers")
- Assigns trait ("naturally empathetic")
- Makes identity claim

**CORRECTED:**
"Situations that pull for Healing engagement—care, repair, presence with difficulty—may feel sustaining for some and costly for others. The capacity to respond to pain is separate from whether doing so feeds or depletes."

## Category 2: Prescriptive Drift

### Example 2.1
**DRIFTED:**
"Based on your MNTEST results, you should consider careers in education or training."

**PROBLEM:**
- Makes prescription ("you should")
- Claims test determines career direction
- Crosses from orientation to direction

**CORRECTED:**
"Your MNTEST results suggest you supply Educative engagement easily. This means work that pulls for explaining and clarifying may feel sustaining. What you do with that information—which contexts to seek, which to avoid—remains yours to decide."

---

### Example 2.2
**DRIFTED:**
"If you're high in Entrepreneurial nature, you need to find work that lets you take initiative and build things."

**PROBLEM:**
- Prescriptive ("you need to")
- Creates necessity from observation

**CORRECTED:**
"If Entrepreneurial engagement flows easily—if initiative and ownership energize you—work that suppresses these may carry cost you feel but can't name. This is orientation: seeing what demands exist and what it costs to meet them. What to do with that clarity is yours."

---

### Example 2.3
**DRIFTED:**
"Your Administrative nature means you'll be happiest in structured environments with clear processes."

**PROBLEM:**
- Predicts happiness
- Creates determination from pattern

**CORRECTED:**
"If you supply Administrative engagement easily—organization, sequencing, follow-through—structured environments may feel less costly than ambiguous ones. This is about cost, not happiness. Many factors determine satisfaction; MN addresses only one."

## Category 3: Transformation/Promise Drift

### Example 3.1
**DRIFTED:**
"Discover your true nature and transform your life with the MNTEST."

**PROBLEM:**
- Promises transformation
- Implies singular "true nature"
- Marketing language that overclaims

**CORRECTED:**
"The MNTEST surfaces patterns you may already recognize but haven't had language for. It shows what situations ask for and what it costs you to meet those demands. What comes after recognition is yours."

---

### Example 3.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Understanding your Multiple Natures will help you find your purpose and live a more fulfilling life."

**PROBLEM:**
- Promises purpose
- Promises fulfillment
- Claims understanding produces outcomes

**CORRECTED:**
"Understanding your engagement patterns may clarify why some contexts feel sustaining and others draining. MN provides orientation—seeing where you are. It does not provide direction or guarantee outcomes."

---

### Example 3.3
**DRIFTED:**
"Unlock your potential by aligning your career with your natural strengths."

**PROBLEM:**
- "Unlock potential" is transformation language
- "Natural strengths" conflates capacity and sustainability
- Implies MN provides the key

**CORRECTED:**
"MN helps you see what different contexts demand and what it costs you to meet those demands. This clarity may inform decisions, but MN does not unlock anything. It shows what is already there."

## Category 4: Strengths/Weaknesses Drift

### Example 4.1
**DRIFTED:**
"Your strength in Interpersonal intelligence means you're great with people."

**PROBLEM:**
- Uses "strength" language
- Collapses capacity with outcome ("great with")
- Identity attribution

**CORRECTED:**
"Strong capacity in Interpersonal Intelligence means you perceive others' states readily and respond to social cues. Whether sustained social engagement feeds or depletes you is a separate question—one MN addresses through the Natures, not the Intelligences."

---

### Example 4.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Focus on your strengths and minimize time spent on your weaknesses."

**PROBLEM:**
- Strengths/weaknesses frame (prohibited)
- Prescriptive advice

**CORRECTED:**
"Some engagements flow easily; others carry cost. What you do with that information—whether to avoid costly engagement or accept it for other reasons—is a choice MN does not make for you."

---

### Example 4.3
**DRIFTED:**
"Your weakness in Administrative engagement means you should avoid operations roles."

**PROBLEM:**
- "Weakness" language
- Prescriptive ("should avoid")

**CORRECTED:**
"If Administrative engagement—organization, sequencing, follow-through—carries high cost for you, operations roles that pull for it consistently may be expensive to sustain. Whether you choose those roles anyway depends on factors beyond MN."

## Category 6: Explanatory Drift

### Example 6.1
**DRIFTED:**
"The reason you struggle with routine tasks is because of your low Administrative nature."

**PROBLEM:**
- Creates causal explanation
- Uses "low" as deficiency
- Labels struggle

**CORRECTED:**
"If sustained Administrative engagement—organization, routine, follow-through—carries cost for you, tasks that pull for it consistently may feel heavier than they look. This describes a pattern, not a cause."

---

### Example 6.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Your child is Entertaining, which explains why they always seek attention."

**PROBLEM:**
- Labels child with identity
- Creates explanation/cause
- "Always" implies permanence

**CORRECTED:**
"If your child supplies Entertaining engagement easily—shaping atmosphere, drawing attention—situations that suppress this may feel restrictive. This describes what appears repeatedly, not who they are."

---

### Example 6.3
**DRIFTED:**
"You're not lazy—you're just not Administratively inclined."

**PROBLEM:**
- Creates identity excuse
- Replaces one label with another
- "Inclined" as trait

**CORRECTED:**
"If Administrative engagement carries cost for you, tasks requiring sustained organization may deplete even when you complete them. This is information about cost, not character."

## Category 7: Gardner Terminology Drift

### Example 7.1
**DRIFTED:**
"Your bodily-kinesthetic intelligence helps you learn through movement."

**PROBLEM:**
- Uses Gardner's undifferentiated term
- Should specify Gross or Fine Bodily

**CORRECTED:**
"Strong Gross Bodily Intelligence means you process through whole-body movement. Strong Fine Bodily Intelligence means you process through precision hand work. These are different channels."

---

### Example 7.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Visual-spatial learners benefit from diagrams and maps."

**PROBLEM:**
- Uses Gardner's undifferentiated term
- Should specify Graphic Visual or Spatial Visual
- "Learners" creates type

**CORRECTED:**
"Those with strong Graphic Visual Intelligence may process 2D representations readily—diagrams, charts, images. Those with strong Spatial Visual Intelligence may navigate 3D space and relationships readily. These overlap but differ."

---

### Example 7.3
**DRIFTED:**
"Her logical-mathematical intelligence makes her good at problem-solving."

**PROBLEM:**
- Uses Gardner's full term (use "Logical" alone)
- "Makes her good at" conflates capacity with outcome

**CORRECTED:**
"Strong Logical Intelligence—pattern recognition, sequential reasoning—provides a channel for certain kinds of problem-solving. Whether she finds sustained analytical work costly is a separate question."

## Category 8: Completeness Drift

### Example 8.1
**DRIFTED:**
"Once you understand your Natures, you'll have a complete picture of who you are."

**PROBLEM:**
- Claims completeness
- Implies MN is comprehensive identity

**CORRECTED:**
"MN addresses one dimension: what situations demand and what it costs you to meet those demands. It does not provide a complete picture of anyone. It provides orientation in one domain."

---

### Example 8.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Multiple Natures plus Multiple Intelligences gives you everything you need to make the right decisions."

**PROBLEM:**
- Claims sufficiency
- "Right decisions" implies MN determines outcomes

**CORRECTED:**
"MI shows how you can work. MN shows what it costs to keep working that way. Together they clarify capacity and sustainability in certain contexts. They do not tell you what decisions to make."

## Category 9: Testimonial Drift

### Example 9.1
**DRIFTED:**
"After taking the MNTEST, I finally found my calling and changed careers. Best decision ever!"

**PROBLEM:**
- Implies causation (test → calling → change)
- Attributes outcome to MN

**CORRECTED:**
"After taking the MNTEST, I had language for patterns I'd always felt but couldn't name. What I did with that recognition was my choice—and the consequences, good and complex, were mine too."

---

### Example 9.2
**DRIFTED:**
"Understanding my Natures transformed my relationship with my partner."

**PROBLEM:**
- Transformation claim
- Implies MN caused relationship change

**CORRECTED:**
"Having language for different engagement patterns helped us talk about differences without blame. What we did with that language—the conversations, the adjustments—was our work, not the framework's."

## The Master Test

Before publishing, run every piece of content through these questions:

1. **Does any sentence create a category of people?** (typing)
2. **Does any sentence tell someone what to do?** (prescription)
3. **Does any sentence promise an outcome?** (transformation)
4. **Does any sentence use Gardner's undifferentiated terms?** (terminology)
5. **Does any sentence claim completeness?** (scope)
6. **Does any sentence create urgency?** (marketing)
7. **Does any sentence explain behavior as caused by Nature?** (causation)

If yes to any, revise before publishing.
