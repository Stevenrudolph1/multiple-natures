## Page-by-Page Specifications

### 1. HOME

**URL:** `/` or `/index`

**Purpose:** First impression. Orient visitors. Route to appropriate section based on who they are and what they need.

**Key Elements:**

**Hero Section:**
- Lead with recognition, not promise
- Core value proposition: "MN names what situations demand, what you supply, and what it costs"
- Avoid urgency or transformation language

**Audience Pathways (clear routing):**
- "I want to understand the framework" → What is MN
- "I want to explore my own patterns" → MN for Individuals / MNTEST
- "I'm a professional who wants to use MN" → MN Training / Affiliation
- "I want to find an MN practitioner" → Directory

**Trust Signals:**
- Steven Rudolph presence (photo, brief intro, link to About)
- Years of development (30+ years)
- Books published
- Practitioners trained
- Countries/contexts where MN is used

**What MN Is / Is Not (brief):**
- Observational framework, not personality typing
- Provides orientation, not direction
- Link to full explanation

**Tone:** Welcoming, clear, measured. Not urgent. Not salesy.

**Length:** Above-fold clarity, scroll for depth. ~500-700 words total.

### 2. WHAT IS MN (Section)

**Length:** 800-1200 words.

#### 2b. The Nine Natures

**URL:** `/nine-natures` or `/what-is-mn/natures`

**Purpose:** Explain the Nine Natures framework without creating types.

**Key Elements:**
- Opening frame: Natures describe situational demands, not identities
- All nine Natures with:
  - Name
  - Brief description of the engagement it describes
  - What situations pull for it
  - Example of easy supply vs. costly supply
- Closing: Everyone can supply all nine; question is cost
- Link to MNTEST

**The Nine Natures:**
1. Protective
2. Educative
3. Administrative
4. Creative
5. Healing
6. Entertaining
7. Providing
8. Entrepreneurial
9. Adventurous

**Critical Canon Compliance:**
- No "[Nature] people are..."
- No career recommendations
- Situational frame throughout

**Tone:** Observational, grounded in examples.

**Length:** 1000-1400 words.

---

#### 2c. The Ten Intelligences

**URL:** `/ten-intelligences` or `/what-is-mn/intelligences`

**Purpose:** Explain MN's modified MI framework.

**Key Elements:**
- The relationship between MI and MN (channel vs. source)
- Why MN modifies Gardner's framework
- The two splits:
  - Bodily-Kinesthetic → Gross Bodily + Fine Bodily
  - Spatial → Graphic Visual + Spatial Visual
- All ten Intelligences with brief descriptions
- What MI doesn't tell you (sustainability, cost)

**The Ten Intelligences:**
1. Gross Bodily
2. Fine Bodily
3. Interpersonal
4. Intrapersonal
5. Logical
6. Linguistic
7. Graphic Visual
8. Spatial Visual

**Critical Canon Compliance:**
- Never use Gardner's original terms
- Intelligences describe capacity, not identity

**Tone:** Educational, precise.

**Length:** 700-1000 words.

### 3. THE MNTEST (Expanded Section)

#### 3a. What Is the MNTEST

**URL:** `/mntest` or `/mntest/overview`

**Purpose:** Comprehensive introduction to what the MNTEST is and does.

**Key Elements:**
- What the MNTEST is (assessment instrument for surfacing engagement patterns)
- What the MNTEST does:
  - Surfaces patterns in how you respond to different situational demands
  - Provides a starting point for observation
  - Offers language for what you may already recognize
  - Highlights what flows easily vs. what costs
- What the MNTEST does NOT do:
  - Does not diagnose
  - Does not predict outcomes

  - Does not tell you what to do
  - Is not a psychometric instrument in the clinical sense
- The role of the MNTEST in the MN framework

**Critical Canon Compliance:**
- No "discover your true self"

- Clear about limits

**Tone:** Inviting but honest.

**Length:** 500-700 words.

#### 5b. Certification vs. Affiliation

**URL:** `/affiliation/certification-vs-affiliation`

**Purpose:** Establish the governing distinction clearly.

**Key Elements:**
- The core distinction:
  - Certification = historical training record (past tense)
  - Affiliation = current naming license (present tense)
- What certification grants (factual: "I completed training")
- What certification does NOT grant (naming rights, MNTEST access, directory listing)
- What affiliation grants (naming rights, directory, MNTEST eligibility)
- The four possible states (certified + affiliated, certified only, affiliated only, neither)
- The grammatical test (past tense vs. present tense)
- Why this distinction exists

**Critical content from MN-AFF-002:**
- Certification is permanent as historical fact
- Affiliation is time-bound, requires renewal
- No grandfather exceptions for naming rights

**Tone:** Clear, factual, not punitive.
