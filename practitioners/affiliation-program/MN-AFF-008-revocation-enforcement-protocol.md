## Purpose

This document defines the **internal protocol** for:

- Identifying affiliation violations
- Responding to misrepresentation
- Escalating enforcement actions
- Revoking affiliation when necessary

This is an internal operations document. It is not shared with practitioners.

---

## GOVERNING PRINCIPLES

### Enforcement Posture

All enforcement actions must be:

- **Quiet** — no public announcements, no shaming
- **Factual** — based on observable language/representation, not opinions
- **Non-emotional** — no personal judgments, no moral framing
- **Proportional** — correction first, revocation last

### What We Enforce

We enforce **representation**, not:

- Practice quality
- Client outcomes
- Personal beliefs
- Methods or techniques
- Business success

### What We Do Not Police

- Ideas
- Interpretations
- Adaptations used privately
- Work done under other names
- Opinions about MN

## PART A — VIOLATION TYPES

### Category 1: Naming Violations (Most Common)

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Using "MN Practitioner" without affiliation | Website says "MN Practitioner" but not affiliated |
| Present-tense MN claims without affiliation | "I practice MN with clients" |
| Using MN branding/logos without affiliation | MN logo on business card |
| Continued MN naming after lapse | Didn't update website after affiliation ended |

**Severity:** Low to Medium
**Response:** Correction request first

### Category 2: Authority Misrepresentation

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Claiming endorsement | "Recommended by Steven Rudolph" |
| Claiming official status | "Official MN Trainer" |
| Claiming certification authority | "I certify MN practitioners" |
| Implying MNI responsibility | "MN guarantees results" |

**Severity:** Medium to High
**Response:** Immediate correction required

### Category 3: Instrument Misuse

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Using MNTEST without affiliation | Accessing MNTEST after lapse |
| Altering MNTEST reports | Removing branding, changing language |
| Reselling MNTEST credits | Sharing credits with others |
| Presenting MNTEST as own instrument | "My proprietary assessment" |

**Severity:** High
**Response:** Access termination + correction

### Category 4: Posture Violations

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Presenting MN as diagnostic | "MNTEST diagnoses your type" |
| Presenting MN as prescriptive | "MN tells you what career to choose" |
| Using MN to label/direct people | "You're a Kinesthetic, so you should..." |

**Severity:** Medium
**Response:** Education + correction request

### Category 5: Reputational Harm

| Violation | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Public attacks on MN/MNI | Defamatory statements |
| Fraudulent use of MN name | Scams using MN branding |
| Associating MN with harmful content | Using MN in discriminatory context |

**Severity:** High
**Response:** May warrant immediate revocation

## PART B — DETECTION

### How Violations Are Identified

| Source | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Self-monitoring | Periodic review of affiliated practitioner materials |
| Third-party reports | Complaints from public or other practitioners |
| Automated alerts | Google alerts, social media monitoring |
| Direct observation | Encountered during normal operations |

### Verification Before Action

Before any enforcement action:

1. **Document the violation** — screenshot, URL, date
2. **Verify affiliation status** — confirm active/lapsed/never affiliated
3. **Assess severity** — categorize per Part A
4. **Check history** — any prior issues with this individual?

Do not act on hearsay. Evidence required.

## PART C — RESPONSE PROCESS

### Level 1: Informal Notice (First Contact)

**Use when:** First-time violation, low severity, likely unintentional

**Method:** Email

**Tone:** Friendly, assumption of good faith

**Template:**

```
Subject: Quick note about your MN representation

Hi [Name],

I noticed [specific issue] on your [website/materials/etc.].

Under MN Affiliation policy, [brief explanation of the rule].

Could you update this to [specific correction needed]?

Happy to clarify if you have questions.

Best,
[Name]
```

**Timeline:** Allow 14 days to correct

**If corrected:** No further action; no record kept beyond internal notes

**If not corrected:** Escalate to Level 2

---

### Level 2: Formal Correction Request

**Use when:** Level 1 ignored, repeat violation, or medium severity

**Method:** Email with read receipt or delivery confirmation

**Tone:** Professional, factual, clear consequences

**Template:**

```
Subject: MN Affiliation — Correction Required

Dear [Name],

This is a formal notice regarding your use of Multiple Natures naming/branding.

Issue identified:
[Specific description with evidence]

Policy reference:
[Citation to MN-AFF-006 or relevant document]

Required action:
[Specific correction needed]

Deadline: [14 days from date]

If not corrected by this date, your affiliation may be subject to revocation per MN-AFF-006 Section 7.

Please confirm receipt and intended action.

Regards,
[Name]
Multiple Natures International
```

**Timeline:** 14 days to correct

**If corrected:** Document resolution; note in file

**If not corrected:** Escalate to Level 3

---

### Level 3: Final Notice (Pre-Revocation)

**Use when:** Level 2 ignored, or high severity violation

**Method:** Email with delivery confirmation

**Tone:** Formal, final

**Template:**

```
Subject: MN Affiliation — Final Notice Before Revocation

Dear [Name],

This is final notice regarding the unresolved matter below.

Original issue:
[Description]

Previous notice sent: [Date]

Despite prior communication, this issue remains unresolved.

If not corrected within 7 days, your MN Affiliation will be revoked effective [date].

Upon revocation:
- All MN naming rights terminate immediately
- Directory listing will be removed
- MNTEST access will be terminated
- You may not reapply for 12 months

This is your final opportunity to resolve this matter.

Regards,
[Name]
Multiple Natures International
```

**Timeline:** 7 days

**If corrected:** Document resolution; issue formal warning

**If not corrected:** Proceed to revocation

---

### Level 4: Revocation

**Use when:** All correction attempts failed, or immediate revocation warranted

**Method:** Email with delivery confirmation

**Template:**

```
Subject: MN Affiliation — Revocation Notice

Dear [Name],

Your MN Affiliation has been revoked effective immediately.

Reason:
[Specific grounds]

Effect:
- All rights to use MN name and marks are terminated
- Your directory listing has been removed
- MNTEST access has been terminated
- Unused credits are forfeited

Per MN-AFF-006 Section 7.3(e), you may not reapply for affiliation for 12 months.

You may continue to reference past training factually (e.g., "Trained in MN in [year]"). You may not use present-tense MN designations or imply current affiliation.

This decision is final.

Regards,
[Name]
Multiple Natures International
```

**Actions upon revocation:**
- Update affiliation database
- Remove from directory
- Terminate MNTEST access
- Forfeit unused credits
- Document in permanent record

## PART D — IMMEDIATE REVOCATION

### When Immediate Revocation Is Warranted

Skip Levels 1-3 and proceed directly to revocation when:

- Fraud involving MN name
- Legal action against MNI arising from practitioner conduct
- Criminal activity associated with MN practice
- Severe reputational harm (clear and documented)
- MNTEST system abuse (hacking, unauthorized access)

### Immediate Revocation Process

1. Document evidence thoroughly
2. Consult with legal counsel if warranted
3. Issue revocation notice immediately
4. Execute all revocation actions (directory, MNTEST, etc.)
5. Document in permanent record

No prior warning required for immediate revocation cases.

## PART E — NON-AFFILIATED VIOLATIONS

### When Someone Uses MN Without Ever Being Affiliated

**Process:**

1. Document the violation
2. Send cease-and-desist style notice (polite first)
3. If ignored, escalate to formal legal notice
4. If still ignored, consult legal counsel for IP enforcement

**Template (First Contact):**

```
Subject: Use of Multiple Natures Name

Dear [Name],

I noticed you are using the Multiple Natures name in [context].

Multiple Natures is a protected name. Public use of the MN name as a professional designation requires affiliation with Multiple Natures International.

Options:
1. Apply for affiliation at [link]
2. Modify your language to reference MN as an influence only (e.g., "informed by MN")

Please update your materials within 14 days.

Happy to discuss if you have questions.

Best,
[Name]
```

## PART F — APPEALS

### Appeal Process

A revoked practitioner may appeal by:

1. Submitting written appeal within 30 days of revocation
2. Stating grounds for appeal
3. Providing any new evidence or context

### Appeal Review

- Appeals reviewed by MNI (Steven Rudolph or designee)
- Decision within 30 days
- Decision is final
- No second appeals

### Grounds for Successful Appeal

- Factual error in violation finding
- Evidence of correction not previously seen
- Extenuating circumstances (medical, etc.)

Appeals based on disagreement with policy will not succeed.

## PART G — RECORD KEEPING

### What to Document

| Event | Documentation Required |
|-------|----------------------|
| Violation identified | Screenshot, URL, date, description |
| Notice sent | Copy of email, date, method |
| Response received | Copy of response, date |
| Correction made | Evidence of correction, date |
| Escalation | Reason, date, next action |
| Revocation | Full record, grounds, date, actions taken |
| Appeal | Appeal text, decision, date |

### Retention

- Active practitioner records: duration of affiliation + 7 years
- Revocation records: indefinite
- Appeal records: indefinite

### Confidentiality

Enforcement records are internal only. We do not:

- Publish revocation lists
- Discuss enforcement with third parties
- Confirm or deny enforcement actions publicly

Exception: legal proceedings may require disclosure.
