## Purpose

This document addresses a specific question:

> "I was certified in MN before affiliation existed. Do the new rules apply to me?"

**Short answer: Yes.**

This document explains why, and what you retain.

---

## The Policy

**Practitioners certified before the introduction of MN Affiliation:**

- Retain their **certification history** (permanent, factual, yours forever)
- Do **not** receive automatic or permanent naming rights
- Must affiliate to use the MN name publicly going forward

**Affiliation is required for ongoing public use of the MN name, regardless of certification date.**

---

## What You Keep (No Matter What)

**Certification is NEVER revoked.** It is a permanent historical record of training you completed. It cannot be taken away — regardless of affiliation status.

You will always be able to say:

✅ "Certified in Multiple Natures (year)"
✅ "Completed MN Practitioner Training in (year)"
✅ "Trained in MN with Steven Rudolph"
✅ "My work is informed by Multiple Natures"

These are factual, past-tense statements. They remain true regardless of affiliation status.

---

## What Requires Affiliation

Present-tense use of the MN name requires affiliation:

❌ "MN Practitioner" → requires affiliation
❌ "I practice Multiple Natures" → requires affiliation
❌ "MN-based coaching" → requires affiliation
❌ MN logo usage → requires affiliation
❌ MNTEST access → requires affiliation

This applies to everyone — including those certified before affiliation existed.

---

## Why No Grandfathering for Naming Rights

### 1. Certification and Affiliation Are Different Things

| | Certification | Affiliation |
|---|---------------|-------------|
| What it covered | Training at a point in time | Ongoing use of the name |
| What it proves | You learned MN | You're authorized to represent as MN now |
| Duration | Permanent record | Time-bound license |

Certification never included perpetual naming rights. This isn't a new restriction — it's a clarification.

### 2. Names Exist in the Present Tense

When someone says "I am an MN Practitioner," that's a current claim.

Current claims require current authorization.

Training completed in 2015 does not authorize present-day representation any more than a 2015 driver's license authorizes driving in 2025.

This follows standard professional practice. A law degree is permanent, but bar membership requires annual renewal. A medical degree is permanent, but licensure requires maintenance. Certification records competence. Affiliation grants current permission to represent.

### 3. Equal Treatment Matters

If early practitioners received permanent naming rights while new practitioners must affiliate, the system would be:

- Unfair to newcomers
- Impossible to enforce consistently
- Confusing to the public

Equal rules for everyone is cleaner, fairer, and more defensible.

### 4. MN Must Remain Verifiable

The public should be able to verify who is currently practicing as MN.

If some people have perpetual naming rights and others don't, verification becomes impossible.

One rule for everyone = one directory that means something.

---

## What This Is NOT

This policy is **not**:

- Punishment for early practitioners
- Revocation of your training (certification is NEVER revoked)
- A judgment on your competence or character
- A restriction on your ideas, methods, or private practice

This is simply a clarification that:

- Naming rights require affiliation
- Affiliation is the same for everyone
- Your training history is untouched

---

## The Fairness Argument (Addressed Directly)

Some may feel: "I was loyal. I trained early. I should be exempt."

We understand this feeling. Here's our response:

**Your early commitment is honored** — your certification stands, your history is real, your contribution to MN is recognized.

**Naming rights are a different matter** — they concern current representation, not past contribution.

We are not asking you to re-earn your training.
We are asking you to maintain current standing if you want current naming rights.

This is the same ask we make of everyone.

## What We Recommend

If you were certified before affiliation existed and want to continue using the MN name:

1. **Apply for affiliation** — [Link to application]
2. **Update your materials** if you choose not to affiliate — shift to past-tense language

Either path is valid. Neither is punished.

## Timeline Consideration

If you are currently using MN naming and need time to decide:

- We will not take immediate enforcement action against legacy practitioners acting in good faith
- We ask that you make a decision within 90 days of this policy's effective date
- After that period, standard enforcement applies

This grace period is a courtesy, not a right — and it applies only to those certified before affiliation was introduced.
