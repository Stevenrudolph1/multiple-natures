## Purpose

This document establishes the **mandatory, irreversible distinction** between MN Certification and MN Affiliation.

This distinction is foundational. All other MN Affiliation documents must align with it.

---

## THE GOVERNING DISTINCTION

| | Certification | Affiliation |
|---|---------------|-------------|
| **What it is** | Historical training record | Current naming license |
| **Tense** | Past | Present |
| **What it proves** | "I completed MN training" | "I am authorized to use the MN name now" |
| **Duration** | Permanent (as historical fact) | Time-bound (annual renewal) |
| **Revocable** | No (history cannot be undone) | Yes (license can lapse or be revoked) |
| **Grants naming rights** | No | Yes |
| **Grants MNTEST access** | No | Yes (eligibility; licensed separately) |
| **Requires payment** | One-time (at time of training) | Ongoing (annual fee) |

---

## WHAT CERTIFICATION GRANTS

Certification is a **factual historical record**.

A certified person may:

- State they completed MN training
- Reference the year of certification
- Describe their learning accurately

**Permitted language (certification only, no affiliation):**

- "Certified in Multiple Natures (2019)"
- "Completed MN Practitioner Training"
- "Trained in MN with Steven Rudolph"
- "My work is informed by Multiple Natures"

Certification does **not** grant:

- Current use of the MN name as a professional designation
- Access to MNTEST
- Listing in the Affiliation Directory
- Permission to present services *as* Multiple Natures

---

## WHAT AFFILIATION GRANTS

Affiliation is a **current, active license**.

An affiliated practitioner may:

- Use "Multiple Natures" as part of their professional identity
- State "MN Practitioner" or equivalent designation
- Present services as MN-based
- Appear in the Official Affiliation Directory
- Access MNTEST (subject to separate licensing)

**Permitted language (with affiliation):**

- "MN Practitioner"
- "Multiple Natures Practitioner"
- "I practice Multiple Natures"
- "MN-based coaching / consulting / guidance"

---

## THE HARD RULE

**Certification ≠ Affiliation**

- Certification without affiliation = past tense only
- Affiliation without certification = permitted (affiliation is about representation, not training history)
- Certification does not automatically convert to affiliation
- No grandfather exceptions for naming rights

---

## FOUR POSSIBLE STATES

| State | Certification | Affiliation | What They May Say |
|-------|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **A** | Yes | Yes | Full MN naming rights |
| **B** | Yes | No | Past training only; no current MN designation |
| **C** | No | Yes | Full MN naming rights (rare but valid) |
| **D** | No | No | May reference MN as influence only |

**State B is the critical case.**

Most disputes will come from practitioners in State B who believe certification entitles them to naming rights. It does not.

---

## GRAMMATICAL TEST

When in doubt, apply this test:

| Language | Tense | Requires Affiliation? |
|----------|-------|----------------------|
| "I was trained in MN" | Past | No |
| "I completed MN certification" | Past | No |
| "I am an MN Practitioner" | Present | **Yes** |
| "I practice MN" | Present | **Yes** |
| "I use MN with clients" | Present | **Yes** |

**Past tense = certification is sufficient.**
**Present tense = affiliation is required.**

---

## WHY THIS DISTINCTION EXISTS

This separation protects:

1. **The public** — They can verify current standing, not just historical training
2. **The field** — MN naming remains meaningful and current
3. **Practitioners** — Clear rules reduce ambiguity and conflict
4. **The founder** — No perpetual obligations from past training

This is standard practice in professional licensing. It is not punitive.

---

## ENFORCEMENT

Violations of this distinction (e.g., using present-tense MN naming without affiliation) are handled per **MN-AFF-008 (Revocation & Enforcement Protocol)**.

Enforcement is:

- Private first
- Factual
- Focused on language correction, not punishment

---

## NON-NEGOTIABLE

This distinction cannot be:

- Waived for individuals
- Modified by side agreements
- Grandfathered for legacy practitioners
- Overridden by certification date or history

It applies equally to everyone.
