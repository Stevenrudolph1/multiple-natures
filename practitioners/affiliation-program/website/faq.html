Practitioner FAQ

Answers to the most common questions about MN Affiliation.

General Questions

What is MN Affiliation?

Affiliation is an annual license to use the Multiple Natures name professionally. It grants you permission to call yourself an "MN Practitioner," appear in the official directory, and access MNTEST. It is not certification, endorsement, or membership. It is naming rights.

Why does affiliation exist?

To maintain clarity about who is currently practicing as MN. Without affiliation, anyone could claim to be an "MN Practitioner," the public couldn't verify practitioner status, and the meaning of "MN Practitioner" would erode over time. Affiliation keeps the name meaningful and verifiable.

How much does it cost?

€300 per year. Multi-year options: 2 years for €550 (save €50), 3 years for €750 (save €150). Installment plans are available for annual affiliation. Full pricing details →

What do I get for the fee?

Permission to use the MN name publicly, listing in the Official Affiliation Directory, eligibility to access MNTEST (purchased separately), and recognition as a current MN Practitioner.

What do I NOT get?

MNTEST credits (purchased separately), courses or training, access to new materials or concepts, certification, or endorsement of your services. The fee is for naming rights only.

Certification vs Affiliation

I'm already certified. Why do I need affiliation?

Certification proves you learned MN at some point. Affiliation proves you're authorized to use the name now. One is history. The other is current status. Learn more →

But I paid for certification. Isn't that enough?

Certification covered your training at that time. Affiliation covers ongoing use of the MN name. These are separate things with separate purposes. This is standard practice in professional fields—a medical degree doesn't grant permanent use of "MD" without maintaining licensure.

Can I still say I was certified?

Yes. You can always state your training history: "Certified in Multiple Natures (2019)," "Completed MN Practitioner Training," "Trained in MN with Steven Rudolph." You just can't use present-tense designations like "MN Practitioner" without affiliation.

What if I was certified before affiliation existed?

The same rules apply. Practitioners certified before the introduction of affiliation retain their certification history (permanent), but do not receive automatic or permanent naming rights. If you want to use the MN name going forward, affiliation is required.

What Can I Say?

What can I say WITHOUT affiliation?

Past-tense and influence language: "Trained in Multiple Natures (2019)," "My work is informed by MN," "I draw on MN concepts in my practice," "Certified in MN (2017)." Full guidance →

What can I say WITH affiliation?

Present-tense MN designations: "MN Practitioner," "Multiple Natures Practitioner," "I practice Multiple Natures," "MN-based coaching."

Can I use the MN logo?

Only with active affiliation. If your affiliation lapses, you must remove the logo.

What if I'm not sure whether my language is okay?

Apply the grammar test: Past tense ("I was trained," "I completed") = OK without affiliation. Present tense ("I am," "I practice") = Requires affiliation. When in doubt, use past tense or contact us.

Payment & Renewal

Is there a payment plan?

Yes. Annual affiliation can be paid in installments (2 or 4 payments). The term is still one year—installments are a payment convenience, not a different product. Missed installment = affiliation lapses.

Is there auto-renewal?

No. Renewal requires explicit action. You'll receive reminders at 60, 30, and 7 days before expiry.

What happens if I don't renew?

Your affiliation lapses at expiry. Immediately: directory listing removed, MNTEST access suspended, naming rights end. You must update your materials to past-tense language.

Is there a grace period?

No. Lapse is immediate at expiry. If you need time, renew early—you can renew up to 60 days before expiry without losing time.

Can I get a refund?

No. Fees are non-refundable once affiliation is activated.

What if I let my affiliation lapse by accident?

You can reinstate by paying the full annual fee. If you reinstate within 30 days: unused MNTEST credits are restored. If after 30 days: credits were forfeited.

MNTEST

Is MNTEST included with affiliation?

No. Affiliation grants eligibility to access MNTEST. Credits are purchased separately.

What happens to my MNTEST credits if I lapse?

Unused credits are forfeited 30 days after lapse. If you reinstate within 30 days, credits are restored. If after 30 days, credits are gone.

Can I use MNTEST without affiliation?

No. MNTEST access requires active affiliation.

Creating Your Own Programs

Can I create my own MN-informed programs?

Yes. You can create courses, workshops, and programs that incorporate MN concepts. You can certify people in YOUR program, charge what you want, and keep 100% of revenue. You cannot certify people as "MN Practitioners," call your program "Official MN" anything, or imply MNI authorization.

Do I need affiliation to create programs?

Not necessarily. If you're only referencing MN as an influence, affiliation is optional. If you're calling yourself "MN Practitioner" or using MNTEST in your program, affiliation is required.

Does MNI take royalties on my programs?

No. MNI does not take royalties, revenue share, or licensing fees. Your program is your business.

Can my graduates call themselves MN Practitioners?

Only if they have their own MN affiliation. Your certificate grants completion of YOUR program—not MN status.

Enforcement

What happens if I use MN naming without affiliation?

You'll receive a private notice asking you to correct your language. We don't publicly shame anyone. Process: private notice, 14 days to correct, escalation only if ignored.

Can affiliation be revoked?

Yes, if you use MN naming without active affiliation, misrepresent MN or imply unauthorized authority, or violate the Terms & Conditions. Revocation follows a notice-and-correction process.

If revoked, can I still practice?

Yes—just not as MN. Revocation affects naming rights only. It does not erase your training history, restrict practice under another name, or prevent you from using MN ideas privately.

Other Questions

Do I have to use MN exactly as taught?

No. You're free to adapt, evolve, and develop your own approach. Affiliation governs naming, not methodology.

What if I disagree with MN or Steven Rudolph?

You're allowed to disagree. Ideas cannot be owned. You can critique MN publicly, develop alternative frameworks, or move in different directions. Just don't claim current MN status while doing so.

Is affiliation exclusive?

No. You can be affiliated with MN and also work with other frameworks, certifications, or approaches.

Can I be affiliated without being certified?

Yes. Affiliation is about current representation, not training history. This is rare but permitted.

What if I just want to use MN ideas privately?

No affiliation needed. Ideas are free. Affiliation is only required for public, professional use of the MN name.

How do I verify if someone is affiliated?

Check the Official Affiliation Directory. If they're listed, they're currently affiliated. If not listed, they're not affiliated (or are using past-tense language appropriately).
