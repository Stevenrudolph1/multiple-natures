## Purpose

This document clarifies what you **can** and **cannot** say about Multiple Natures if you are not currently affiliated.

It exists to:

- Reduce confusion
- Prevent unintentional violations
- Respect your training history while maintaining naming clarity

---

## Core Principle

**Ideas are free. Names are not.**

You may use MN concepts freely.
You may not present yourself as an MN Practitioner without affiliation.

---

## Who This Applies To

This guidance applies to anyone who:

- Was previously affiliated but is no longer
- Completed MN certification but never affiliated
- Learned MN concepts informally
- Uses MN ideas in their work but hasn't formalized the relationship

**Important:** If you completed certification, that certification is NEVER revoked. It is a permanent historical record. What changes when affiliation ends is naming rights — not your training history.

---

## What You CAN Do (Without Affiliation)

### Reference Your Training History

You may state factual history:

✅ "I completed MN Practitioner certification in 2019"
✅ "I trained in Multiple Natures with Steven Rudolph"
✅ "I studied the Multiple Natures framework"
✅ "Certified in Multiple Natures (2017)"

These are **past-tense, factual statements**. They describe what happened, not what you are now.

### Describe MN as an Influence

You may acknowledge MN's influence on your work:

✅ "My work is informed by Multiple Natures"
✅ "I draw on MN concepts in my practice"
✅ "Influenced by the Multiple Natures framework"
✅ "My approach incorporates ideas from MN"

These statements do not claim current MN status.

### Use MN Concepts Privately

You may:

✅ Think with MN concepts
✅ Use MN ideas in private reflection
✅ Apply MN thinking in conversations (without claiming MN practitioner status)
✅ Recommend MN books or resources to others

### Evolve Beyond MN

You may:

✅ Develop your own methods inspired by MN
✅ Create frameworks that build on MN concepts
✅ Move in different directions from MN
✅ Critique or disagree with MN publicly

Ideas cannot be owned. Your intellectual freedom is not restricted.

---

## What You CANNOT Do (Without Affiliation)

### Claim Current MN Status

You may not use present-tense MN designations:

❌ "MN Practitioner"
❌ "Multiple Natures Practitioner"
❌ "I practice Multiple Natures"
❌ "I am an MN coach/consultant"
❌ "Certified MN Practitioner" (as a current title)

### Present Services as MN-Based

You may not market services as MN:

❌ "MN Coaching"
❌ "Multiple Natures sessions available"
❌ "Using MN with clients"
❌ "Book an MN consultation"

### Use MN Branding

You may not use:

❌ MN logo
❌ Multiple Natures wordmark
❌ Official MN graphics or templates
❌ MNTEST branding

### Access MNTEST

You may not:

❌ Purchase MNTEST credits
❌ Administer MNTEST assessments
❌ Deliver MNTEST reports

MNTEST access requires active affiliation.

### Imply Current Standing

You may not suggest current authorization:

❌ "Official MN practitioner"
❌ "Authorized to deliver MN"
❌ "Endorsed by Multiple Natures"
❌ "Part of the MN network"

---

## The Grammar Rule

When in doubt, apply this test:

| Statement | Tense | Affiliation Required? |
|-----------|-------|----------------------|
| "I was trained in MN" | Past | No |
| "I completed MN certification" | Past | No |
| "My work is informed by MN" | Descriptive | No |
| "I am an MN Practitioner" | Present | **Yes** |
| "I practice MN" | Present | **Yes** |
| "I use MN with clients" | Present | **Yes** |

**Past tense and descriptive influence = OK**
**Present-tense designation = requires affiliation**

## Examples: Acceptable Website Language

If you are **not affiliated**, your website might say:

> "I trained in Multiple Natures in 2019 and continue to draw on its insights in my coaching practice."

> "My approach is informed by several frameworks, including Multiple Natures."

> "Background: Certified in Multiple Natures (2018), ICF-ACC, MA Psychology"

These are factual, historical, and do not claim current MN status.

## Examples: Not Acceptable (Without Affiliation)

These would require affiliation:

> "As an MN Practitioner, I help clients discover their strengths."

> "Services: MN-based career coaching"

> "I'm a certified Multiple Natures Practitioner ready to guide your journey."

The issue: present-tense claims of MN practitioner status.

## Why This Matters

This isn't about restricting ideas. It's about:

- **Clarity for the public** — People can verify who is currently practicing as MN
- **Fairness to affiliated practitioners** — They maintain their status; this protects its meaning
- **Honesty in representation** — Past training is not the same as current authorization

This follows standard professional practice. A law degree is permanent, but bar membership requires annual renewal. A medical degree is permanent, but licensure requires maintenance. Certification records competence. Affiliation grants current permission to represent.

## Questions

**Q: Can I say "Certified MN Practitioner" on my LinkedIn?**
A: Only if you're currently affiliated. Otherwise, say "Certified in MN (year)" — past tense.

**Q: Can I teach MN concepts in my own courses?**
A: You can reference MN as an influence. You cannot certify others in MN or present your course as official MN training.

**Q: What if I let my affiliation lapse by accident?**
A: Update your materials to past-tense language, or reinstate your affiliation. [Reinstatement info]

**Q: Can I use MN ideas without mentioning MN at all?**
A: Yes. Ideas are free. You can use the concepts without naming them.

**Q: What happens if I use MN naming without affiliation?**
A: You'll receive a private notice asking you to correct your language. We don't publicly shame anyone — we just maintain the boundary.
