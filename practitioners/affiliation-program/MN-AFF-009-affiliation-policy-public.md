## What Is MN Affiliation?

MN Affiliation is a **current, active license** to use the Multiple Natures name professionally.

It is:

- An annual, renewable agreement
- Permission to present yourself as an MN Practitioner
- A public signal that your MN status is current and verifiable

It is **not**:

- Certification (that's a separate, historical record)
- Endorsement of your services
- A guarantee of quality or outcomes
- Ownership of MN ideas

---

## Core Principle

**Ideas are free. Names are not.**

Anyone may use MN concepts, think with them, adapt them, or move beyond them.

Only affiliated practitioners may publicly present their work **as Multiple Natures**.

---

## What Affiliation Grants

As an affiliated practitioner, you may:

- Use "Multiple Natures" in your professional identity
- Call yourself an "MN Practitioner"
- Be listed in the [Official Affiliation Directory](#)
- Access MNTEST (licensed separately)
- Reference MN in your website, materials, and client work

---

## What Affiliation Requires

As an affiliated practitioner, you agree to:

- Maintain active affiliation (annual renewal)
- Represent MN accurately
- Not claim endorsement or authority beyond what affiliation grants
- Not present MN as a diagnostic, prescriptive, or labeling system
- Remove MN naming if your affiliation ends

## Certification vs Affiliation

These are different things.

| | Certification | Affiliation |
|---|---------------|-------------|
| What it is | Training you completed | License to use the name now |
| Tense | Past | Present |
| Duration | Permanent record | Annual renewal |
| Revocable | **No — certification is NEVER revoked** | Yes |
| Grants naming rights | No | Yes |

This follows standard professional practice: a law degree is permanent, but bar membership requires annual renewal to practice. A medical degree is permanent, but licensure requires maintenance. Certification records competence. Affiliation grants current permission to represent.

**Certified but not affiliated?**
You may say: "Trained in Multiple Natures (year)"
You may not say: "MN Practitioner"

**Can my certification be revoked?**
No. Certification is a permanent historical record. It documents training you completed and can never be taken away. Only affiliation (current naming rights) can lapse or be revoked.

MNTEST is the official MN assessment instrument.

- Affiliation grants **eligibility** to use MNTEST
- MNTEST is licensed separately (pay-per-use)
- No affiliation = no MNTEST access

## If You're Not Affiliated

You may still:

- Reference MN as an influence on your work
- State your training history factually
- Use MN concepts privately

You may not:

- Call yourself an "MN Practitioner"
- Present services as MN-based
- Use MN branding or logos
- Access MNTEST

Full guidance: [Non-Affiliated Use Guidance (MN-AFF-010)](#)

---

## Renewal & Lapse

- Affiliation renews annually
- You'll receive reminders at 60, 30, and 7 days before expiry
- Renewal requires explicit action (no auto-renewal)
- If you don't renew, affiliation lapses immediately
- Lapsed = removed from directory, MNTEST suspended, naming rights end

---

## Revocation

Affiliation may be revoked if you:

- Use MN naming without active affiliation
- Misrepresent MN or imply unauthorized authority
- Violate the Terms & Conditions

Revocation process:

1. Private notice
2. Opportunity to correct (14 days)
3. Revocation if unresolved

Revocation affects naming rights only. It does not erase your training history or restrict your practice under another name.
