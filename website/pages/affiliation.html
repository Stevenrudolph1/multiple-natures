Affiliation Program

A naming license for professionals who practice Multiple Natures. Current, time-bound, and renewed annually.

The MN Affiliation Program is for professionals who want to use Multiple Natures in their work and identify themselves as MN practitioners.

Affiliation is not simply recognition of past training. It is a current relationship—a license to use the MN name in the present tense. "I am an MN Practitioner." "I practice Multiple Natures." These statements require affiliation.

The distinction matters. Training is something you completed. Affiliation is something you maintain. One is past tense; the other is present.

What affiliation is

Affiliation is a naming license. It grants permission to use the Multiple Natures name in describing your current practice.

Affiliation is time-bound. It runs for one year and requires renewal. This is not a bureaucratic burden—it reflects the nature of the relationship. Professional practice in MN is ongoing, not a one-time event.

Affiliation is current. It means you are practicing MN now, not that you once learned it. The present-tense framing is essential.

What affiliation grants

Naming rights. Affiliates can say "I am an MN Practitioner," "I practice Multiple Natures," and similar present-tense statements about their work.

Directory listing. Affiliates are listed in the Practitioner Directory, making them findable by people seeking MN practitioners.

MN Mapper eligibility. Affiliates are eligible to license the MN Mapper (formerly MNTEST) for use with their clients (MN Mapper licensing is separate from affiliation).

Ongoing support. Affiliates have access to community, updates, and continued development as the framework evolves.

The relationship to training

Training and affiliation are distinct but connected.

Training is where you learn. It is a learning experience where you develop understanding and skill in using MN. Completing training results in certification—a permanent record that is NEVER revoked.

Affiliation is where you practice. It is a licensing relationship that allows you to use MN professionally and identify as an MN practitioner. Affiliation is renewed annually.

This follows standard professional practice. A law degree is permanent, but bar membership requires annual renewal. A medical degree is permanent, but licensure requires maintenance. Certification records competence. Affiliation grants current permission to represent.

You can be trained but not affiliated—if you completed training but have not applied for affiliation, or if you were once affiliated but allowed your affiliation to lapse. In these cases, you can speak about your training in past tense, but you cannot identify as a current practitioner. Your certification remains permanently intact either way.

Who affiliation is for

Affiliation is for professionals who want to:

Use MN with clients, students, or teams—and want to be clear about their training and standing when they do.

Identify publicly as MN practitioners—on their website, in their materials, in professional contexts.

Use the MN Mapper professionally—which requires both affiliation and separate MN Mapper licensing.

Be listed in the Practitioner Directory—so people seeking MN practitioners can find them.

Stay connected to the MN community—receiving updates, participating in ongoing development, maintaining the relationship over time.

How affiliation works

To become affiliated, you apply after completing MN Training. Applications are reviewed, and if approved, you receive your affiliation for one year.

Affiliation is renewed annually. Before your affiliation year ends, you complete a renewal process. This maintains your standing and continues your naming rights, directory listing, and other affiliate benefits.

If you choose not to renew, or if renewal is not completed, your affiliation lapses. You can still speak about your past training, but you can no longer identify as a current practitioner.

Posture expectation

Affiliation implies respect for thresholding: the posture that governs when action becomes admissible.

This means recognizing that advice, interpretation, or intervention can arrive too early—before the situation is clear, before action has a legitimate place. It means understanding that non-action is a complete and valid endpoint, not a failure.

Affiliates are not required to prove they hold the posture, report on their practice, or follow specific methods. MN does not police, audit, or enforce. The posture is held, not checked.

What affiliation asks is orientation, not compliance: that your work does not publicly contradict the posture while claiming MN alignment.

The governing principle

You are free to do your work. If you want to do it as Multiple Natures, these are the terms.

Ideas are free. Names are not. Anyone may use MN concepts privately, think with them, or describe their work as "informed by MN." Only affiliated practitioners may publicly represent their work as Multiple Natures.
