MN Training

A live cohort learning experience for professionals who want to use Multiple Natures accurately and within its limits.

MN Training is for people who want to use the framework professionally—educators, coaches, counselors, HR professionals, and others who work with people in contexts where understanding engagement patterns would be valuable.

The training is not self-paced. It runs as a live cohort, with real-time sessions where participants learn together, practice together, and receive feedback together. The format reflects the nature of the work: learning to see accurately requires correction in the moment, not review after the fact.

The goal is competence, not completion. By the end of training, participants should be able to use MN accurately, recognize when they're drifting into overclaim, and stop well.

What training is

MN Training is a learning experience. It is where you develop the understanding and skill to use MN in your professional context.

The training covers the framework deeply—the Nine Natures, the Ten Intelligences, the demand/supply/cost model, and how they work together. It covers the MN Mapper (formerly MNTEST)—how to use it accurately, how to interpret results, how to communicate about results without overclaiming.

Critically, the training covers what MN cannot do. Learning the limits is not separate from learning the framework. It is the framework. Knowing where to stop is the competence.

Training includes practice. You will work with the framework, receive feedback, make corrections, and try again. The learning is active, not passive.

What training is not

Training is not affiliation. Completing training does not automatically make you an affiliated practitioner. Training is learning; affiliation is a separate licensing relationship.

Completing training makes you eligible to apply for affiliation. Most who complete training do affiliate. But the two are distinct.

Training is also not certification in the sense of a permanent credential that grants ongoing rights. What you learn is yours to keep. But ongoing use of MN professionally—identifying yourself as an MN practitioner, using the MN Mapper with clients—requires affiliation, which is renewed annually.

What training leads to

Completing training gives you:

Deep understanding of the MN framework. You will know the Nine Natures, the Ten Intelligences, and the demand/supply/cost model well enough to recognize patterns accurately.

Skill in using the MN Mapper. You will be able to interpret results, communicate about them clearly, and avoid the common errors that turn observation into prescription.

Clarity about limits. You will know what MN can and cannot do. You will recognize when you're crossing from observation to overclaim. You will know when to stop.

Eligibility for affiliation. If you choose to practice MN professionally, training is the prerequisite for applying to the Affiliation Program.

Who training is for

Training is designed for professionals who work with people and want to add MN as a lens in their practice:

Educators who want language for what they observe in classrooms. Coaches who want to help clients see patterns in engagement and cost. Counselors and therapists who want an observational tool that respects limits. HR professionals who want to understand team dynamics without sorting or predicting.

Training is also for anyone who wants to use MN professionally, whatever their context.

How training works

Training runs as a live cohort. Sessions are scheduled in advance, and participants attend together in real time. Between sessions, there is work to complete—reading, reflection, practice.

The live format allows for immediate feedback. When someone drifts into typing language or prescriptive framing, they can be corrected in the moment. When someone struggles to articulate a pattern, they can get help immediately. This kind of learning doesn't happen in self-paced formats.
