Affiliation FAQ

Common questions about MN affiliation, training requirements, naming rights, and the path to becoming an affiliate.

What is the difference between certification and affiliation?

Certification is a historical training record. It documents that you completed MN Training at a specific time. It is permanent—certification is NEVER revoked.

Affiliation is a current naming license. It grants permission to use Multiple Natures language in the present tense. It runs for one year and must be renewed.

The practical test is grammatical. Certification allows past-tense statements ("I was certified in MN"). Affiliation allows present-tense statements ("I am an MN Practitioner").

This follows standard professional practice: a law degree is permanent, but bar membership requires annual renewal. Certification records competence. Affiliation grants current permission to represent.

For a detailed explanation, see certification vs. affiliation.

Can my certification be revoked?

No. Certification is NEVER revoked. It is a permanent historical record of training you completed. That fact cannot be taken away.

Only affiliation can lapse (if not renewed) or be revoked (for violations). Certification remains intact regardless of what happens with affiliation.

If your affiliation ends, you can still truthfully say: "I completed MN certification in [year]." What you cannot say is "I am an MN Practitioner"—that requires current affiliation.

Do I need to complete training before I can affiliate?

Yes. Training is a prerequisite for affiliation.

Training is where you learn the framework. Affiliation is where you practice it. You cannot affiliate without first being trained.

If you completed training years ago, that still counts. There is no requirement to retrain before affiliating.

What can I call myself if I am affiliated?

As an affiliate, you can use present-tense MN language:

"I am an MN Practitioner."
"I practice Multiple Natures."
"I offer MN-based coaching."
"I use MN in my work with clients."

These statements describe current practice. They require affiliation.

What can I call myself if I am not affiliated?

If you completed training but are not currently affiliated, you can use past-tense language:

"I completed MN certification in [year]."
"I was certified in Multiple Natures."
"I trained in MN."
"My work is informed by MN."

You cannot use present-tense practitioner language. "I am an MN Practitioner" requires current affiliation.

For detailed guidance, see non-affiliated use guidance.

Does affiliation give me access to the MN Mapper?

Affiliation makes you eligible to license the MN Mapper (formerly MNTEST). The license itself is separate.

If you want to use the MN Mapper with clients, you need both affiliation and an MN Mapper license. Affiliation is the prerequisite; the license is the additional step.

Non-affiliates cannot license the MN Mapper, regardless of their training history.

What happens if my affiliation lapses?

If your affiliation expires and you do not renew:

Your naming rights end. You can no longer use present-tense MN language.

Your directory listing is removed.

Your MN Mapper license (if you have one) becomes inactive.

Your certification remains intact. Certification is NEVER affected—it is a permanent historical record that cannot be taken away.

There is no penalty for letting affiliation lapse. You can re-affiliate at any time.

Can I re-affiliate after my affiliation has lapsed?

Yes. You can re-affiliate at any time, regardless of how long your affiliation has been lapsed.

The process is similar to initial application. You complete the application form, confirm your training history, and pay the current annual fee.

There is no penalty period and no additional requirements. Your prior affiliation history is noted but does not affect your new affiliation terms.

I was certified years ago. Do I need to retrain to affiliate?

No. If you completed training previously, that training still counts as the prerequisite for affiliation.

The requirement is completion of MN Training, not recent training. Whether you trained last year or ten years ago, you are eligible to apply for affiliation.

You may find it helpful to review current materials as part of returning to practice, but this is not a formal requirement.

Is affiliation annual or one-time?

Affiliation is annual. It runs for one year and must be renewed to continue.

This reflects standard professional practice. Lawyers must maintain bar membership. Doctors must maintain licensure. Coaches renew professional affiliations. Certification records competence; affiliation grants current permission to represent.

Annual renewal does not require re-certification, re-examination, or re-training. It is an administrative renewal, not a re-qualification.

What is thresholding?

Thresholding is the posture that governs when action becomes admissible. It tends the boundary at which advice, interpretation, or intervention becomes appropriate—rather than crossing too early.

Thresholding is not a method, technique, or skill. It cannot be taught, practiced, or measured. It is a posture—a way of standing in relation to action.

Affiliation implies respect for this posture. See Posture for more.

Does MN check whether I hold the thresholding posture correctly?

No. There is no thresholding compliance.

MN does not monitor, audit, or supervise affiliates. MN does not evaluate how affiliates hold the posture, require proof of practice, or provide feedback on performance.

The posture expectation is stated. Whether and how an affiliate holds the posture is their own matter.

The only boundary mechanism is non-representation: if an affiliate's public positioning explicitly contradicts the posture while claiming MN alignment, they may be removed from listing. This is the sole consequence—and it is not policing, judgment, or correction.
