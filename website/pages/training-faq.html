Training FAQ

Common questions about MN Training, what it leads to, and how it relates to professional MN practice.

What is the difference between training and affiliation?

Training is a learning experience. Affiliation is an ongoing professional relationship.

Training is where you develop the understanding and skill to use MN accurately. You learn the framework, practice using it, receive feedback, and demonstrate competence. Training has a beginning and an end.

Affiliation is a licensing relationship that allows you to identify as an MN Practitioner, use the MN Mapper (formerly MNTEST) professionally, and be listed in the Practitioner Directory. Affiliation is renewed annually and represents current professional standing.

Completing training makes you eligible to apply for affiliation. Most people who complete training do affiliate, but the two are distinct. Training teaches you MN; affiliation authorizes professional use.

See certification vs. affiliation for full details on this distinction.

Do I need prior experience with MN?

No. Training does not require prior experience with Multiple Natures. You do not need to have taken the MN Mapper. You do not need prior exposure to the Nine Natures, the Ten Intelligences, or any other component of the framework.

Training covers everything from the ground up. If you have prior familiarity with MN, that context can help, but lack of it is not a barrier to enrollment or success.

What matters more than prior knowledge is orientation: comfort with observation over prescription, tolerance for limits, willingness to learn what not to do as much as what to do.

What if I miss a session?

Training runs as a live cohort with real-time sessions. The live format is essential—it allows for immediate feedback and correction that cannot happen asynchronously.

That said, life happens. Limited absence accommodation may be possible depending on circumstances and what is missed. If you know in advance that you will miss a session, let us know as early as possible.

Some content can be partially recovered through recordings or materials. But the practice and feedback portions of sessions cannot be fully recovered. If you miss significant portions of training, you may need to complete those portions in a future cohort.

Specific attendance policies are provided with enrollment. If you anticipate scheduling conflicts with an upcoming cohort, consider waiting for a cohort whose schedule works better.

Is there a certification at the end?

Completing training results in certification—a record that you completed MN Training at a specific time. This is a permanent historical record. Certification is NEVER revoked—it cannot be taken away.

Certification allows you to speak about your training in past tense: "I completed MN certification." "I was trained in Multiple Natures." "My work is informed by MN."

Certification alone does not authorize present-tense professional use: "I am an MN Practitioner." "I practice Multiple Natures." For this, you need affiliation, which is separate from and in addition to training certification.

This follows standard professional practice: a law degree is permanent, but bar membership requires annual renewal. Certification records competence. Affiliation grants current permission to represent.

The distinction between certification (historical training record) and affiliation (current naming license) is explained fully in certification vs. affiliation.

What can I do after completing training?

After completing training, several paths are available.

Apply for affiliation. If you want to practice MN professionally—identifying as an MN Practitioner, using the MN Mapper with clients, being listed in the Practitioner Directory—you can apply for affiliation. Training completion is a prerequisite.

Use your learning informally. The understanding you gain from training is yours to keep. You can use MN concepts to inform your thinking, your work, your understanding of patterns—without formally identifying as an MN Practitioner or using the MN Mapper professionally.

Decide later. You do not have to choose immediately after training. You can complete training, take time to integrate what you learned, and decide about affiliation when you are ready.

What you cannot do without affiliation: identify yourself as a current MN Practitioner, use MN-branded language in present tense, use the MN Mapper professionally, or be listed in the Practitioner Directory.

How do I affiliate after completing training?

Affiliation is a separate process from training. After completing training, you are eligible to apply.

The affiliation application is straightforward for training completers. You are not re-demonstrating competence; that was established in training. The application confirms your intention to practice MN professionally and establishes the ongoing relationship.

Affiliation includes an annual fee, separate from the training investment. Affiliation is renewed annually. Details are provided in the affiliation section.

Most people who complete training apply for affiliation immediately or shortly after. But you can also wait—training completion does not expire, and you can apply for affiliation whenever you are ready to begin professional practice.

Can I use MN before completing training?

You can use MN concepts informally before completing training—in your thinking, in conversations, in your own self-understanding. The ideas are available to anyone who reads about them.

What you cannot do without training and affiliation: identify yourself as an MN Practitioner, use MN professionally with clients, or use the MN Mapper in your professional practice.

This is not just about authorization. Using MN accurately requires skill that training develops. Without that skill, it is easy to drift into typing, prescription, and overclaim—the very things MN is designed to avoid. Training teaches you not just what MN is but how to use it without undermining what makes it valuable.

What if I complete training but do not affiliate?

Completing training without affiliating is a legitimate choice. Not everyone who completes training intends to practice MN professionally.

If you complete training but do not affiliate:

You keep everything you learned. The understanding is yours. You can use MN concepts to inform your thinking and your work informally.

You can speak about your training in past tense. "I completed MN Training." "I was certified in Multiple Natures." "My approach is informed by MN." These statements are accurate and appropriate.

You cannot identify as a current MN Practitioner. "I am an MN Practitioner" requires affiliation. The grammar matters—present tense claims require current affiliation.

You cannot use the MN Mapper professionally. Access to the MN Mapper for client use requires both affiliation and MN Mapper licensing.

You will not be listed in the Practitioner Directory. The directory lists current affiliates, not everyone who has been trained.

You can affiliate later. Training completion does not expire. Whenever you are ready to practice professionally, you can apply for affiliation.

What if I affiliate and later let it lapse?

If you affiliate after training but later do not renew your affiliation—whether by choice or by not completing renewal—your status changes.

Your certification remains permanently intact. Certification is NEVER revoked—it is a historical record that cannot be taken away. You can still speak about your training in past tense.

You can no longer use present-tense MN language. "I am an MN Practitioner" becomes inaccurate. "I was an MN Practitioner" is accurate.

Your MN Mapper licensing becomes inactive. You can no longer use the MN Mapper with clients.

You are removed from the Practitioner Directory.

The path back is clear. If you want to restore professional standing, you apply for re-affiliation. The training you completed is still valid; you do not need to retrain, recertify, or re-examine.

How much does training cost?

Training investment varies by cohort and is listed with each scheduled training. The investment covers all live sessions, materials, between-session work, and feedback throughout the program.

Training investment is separate from affiliation fees. Completing training makes you eligible for affiliation; affiliation has its own annual fee.

Payment plans may be available. Details are provided with specific cohort listings.

See schedule and enrollment for current pricing and cohort information.

How long is training?

Training duration varies by cohort format. Some cohorts are more intensive with sessions closer together; others are spread over a longer period.

Total time commitment includes both live sessions and between-session work. The between-session work is substantial—reading, reflection, and practice assignments that prepare you for each session.

Specific schedules and time commitments are listed with each cohort. Review the schedule carefully before enrolling to ensure it works with your other commitments.

What happens if I start training and cannot complete it?

If circumstances prevent you from completing a cohort, accommodation depends on timing and nature of the situation.

If you need to stop early in training, you may be able to transfer to a future cohort without additional cost. If you stop later in training, partial credit may be available toward a future cohort.

Specific policies are provided with enrollment. If you anticipate significant life events or scheduling uncertainties, consider discussing them before enrolling.
