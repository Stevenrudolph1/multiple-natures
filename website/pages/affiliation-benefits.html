Benefits of Affiliation

What affiliation provides to MN practitioners. Naming rights, directory listing, MN Mapper eligibility, community, and ongoing updates.

Affiliation is the relationship that allows professionals to practice Multiple Natures and identify themselves as MN practitioners. The benefits are practical and direct.

Naming rights

The primary benefit of affiliation is the right to use Multiple Natures language in the present tense.

As an affiliate, you can say:

"I am an MN Practitioner."
"I practice Multiple Natures."
"I offer MN-based coaching."
"I use MN with my clients."

These statements claim current practice. Without affiliation, only past-tense statements are appropriate ("I completed MN training," "I was certified in MN").

This distinction matters. When someone identifies as an MN Practitioner, it should mean they are actively practicing now, not that they once learned the framework.

Practitioner Directory listing

Affiliates are listed in the Practitioner Directory.

The directory is the public registry of people currently practicing MN. When someone seeks an MN practitioner, the directory is where they look.

Your listing includes your name, location, areas of focus, and contact information. You control what information appears.

Non-affiliates are not listed. The directory reflects current practitioners, not historical trainees.

MN Mapper eligibility

Affiliates are eligible to license the MN Mapper for use with their clients.

The MN Mapper is the assessment tool that measures the Nine Natures. Using it professionally requires two things: affiliation status and a separate MN Mapper license.

Affiliation is the prerequisite. It establishes that you are a current, practicing professional. The MN Mapper license itself is a separate agreement with its own terms.

Non-affiliates cannot license the MN Mapper, regardless of their training history.

Community and support

Affiliates have access to the MN practitioner community.

This includes connection to other practitioners, opportunities for peer consultation, and access to shared resources and discussions.

The community is not a formal structure with scheduled events. It is a network of professionals using the same framework. Being part of it means you can reach out, ask questions, and contribute to the ongoing development of practice.

Ongoing updates

Multiple Natures is a living framework. It evolves as understanding deepens and as practitioners develop new applications.

Affiliates receive updates as the framework develops. This includes refinements to the theory, new guidance on practice, and developments in how MN is applied across different contexts.

Staying connected ensures your practice reflects current understanding, not just what was taught at a particular point in time.

What affiliation does not include

Affiliation is a naming license and community membership. It does not include:

MN Mapper licensing. The test is licensed separately. Affiliation makes you eligible; it does not grant the license.

Training. Affiliation follows training. It does not replace or include additional training.

Certification of competence. Affiliation is not an endorsement of skill level. It indicates you are a current practitioner with standing to use the MN name, not that your practice has been evaluated.
