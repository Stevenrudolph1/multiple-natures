# Prohibited Patterns Reference

**Detailed AI Anti-Drift Rules**

---

## Purpose

This document provides explicit rules for AI systems generating MN content. These are hard constraints—violations should trigger revision, not judgment calls.

---

## Section 1: Absolute Prohibitions

These patterns must NEVER appear in any MN content.

### 1.1 Identity Typing

**NEVER generate:**
- "You are a [Nature]"
- "You are [Nature]"
- "As a [Nature] person"
- "[Nature]s are..."
- "[Nature] people..."
- "Those who are [Nature]..."
- "Your [Nature] nature"
- "Being [Nature] means..."

**Detection pattern:**
Any sentence where a Nature word is used as a noun referring to a person or category of people.

**Correction:**
Replace with situational framing:
- "You supply [Nature] engagement easily"
- "If [Nature] engagement flows easily for you"
- "Those who supply [Nature] engagement easily may..."

---

### 1.2 Prescriptive Advice

**NEVER generate:**
- "You should..."
- "You need to..."
- "You must..."
- "Try to..."
- "Make sure you..."
- "Consider [doing X]" (when framed as advice)
- "It's important that you..."
- "The best thing for you is..."

**Detection pattern:**
Any sentence that tells the reader what action to take based on their MN profile.

**Correction:**
Replace with information + returned agency:
- "What you do with this information is yours to decide"
- "Some people use this to... Others..."
- Remove prescriptive sentence entirely

---

### 1.3 Transformation Promises

**NEVER generate:**
- "Transform your life"
- "Change your life"
- "Unlock your potential"
- "Find your purpose"
- "Discover your true self"
- "Become who you're meant to be"
- "Live your best life"
- "Achieve your dreams"
- "Reach your full potential"

**Detection pattern:**
Any promise of positive life outcome attributed to MN.

**Correction:**
Replace with accurate scope:
- "See patterns more clearly"
- "Find language for what you already recognize"
- "Understand what situations demand"

---

### 1.4 Prediction Claims

**NEVER generate:**
- "MN predicts..."
- "Your results indicate you will..."
- "This means you'll be successful at..."
- "You're likely to..."
- "You're destined for..."
- "Your future in [field]..."
- "You're meant to..."

**Detection pattern:**
Any forward-looking claim about outcomes based on MN.

**Correction:**
Replace with present-tense observation:
- "Your results suggest [pattern]"
- "This may clarify why [current experience]"
- Remove prediction entirely

---

### 1.5 Career/Relationship Matching

**NEVER generate:**
- "Best careers for [Nature]"
- "Ideal jobs for..."
- "You should work in..."
- "Your perfect partner is..."
- "Compatible Natures..."
- "[Nature]s thrive in..."
- "Avoid careers in..."

**Detection pattern:**
Any suggestion that MN determines career or relationship fit.

**Correction:**
Replace with cost-frame:
- "Work that pulls for [Nature] engagement may feel less costly if you supply it easily"
- Remove matching claim entirely

---

### 1.6 Gardner's Original Terminology

**NEVER generate:**
- "Bodily-kinesthetic intelligence"
- "Visual-spatial intelligence"
- "Logical-mathematical intelligence"
- "Spatial intelligence" (alone, undifferentiated)

**Detection pattern:**
Any use of Gardner's original terminology instead of MN's modified terms.

**Correction:**
Replace with MN terms:
- "Bodily-kinesthetic" → "Gross Bodily" or "Fine Bodily" (specify which)
- "Visual-spatial" → "Graphic Visual" or "Spatial Visual" (specify which)
- "Logical-mathematical" → "Logical"
- "Spatial" (alone) → "Graphic Visual" or "Spatial Visual"

---

### 1.7 Completeness Claims

**NEVER generate:**
- "Complete picture of who you are"
- "Everything you need to know"
- "Comprehensive understanding"
- "Total self-knowledge"
- "Full picture of your personality"

**Detection pattern:**
Any claim that MN provides comprehensive self-knowledge.

**Correction:**
Replace with bounded scope:
- "MN addresses one dimension: what situations demand and what it costs"
- "MN provides orientation in one domain"

---

### 1.8 Urgency Language

**NEVER generate:**
- "Don't wait"
- "Act now"
- "Limited time"
- "Don't miss out"
- "Before it's too late"
- "Start today"
- "Sign up now"
- "Hurry"

**Detection pattern:**
Any language creating time pressure for action.

**Correction:**
Replace with permission-granting:
- "Take it when you're ready"
- "Explore at your own pace"
- Remove urgency language entirely

---

### 1.9 Strengths/Weaknesses Frame

**NEVER generate:**
- "Your strengths are..."
- "Your weaknesses are..."
- "Strength in [Nature]"
- "Weakness in [Nature]"
- "Low [Nature]"
- "High [Nature]" (when framed as trait)

**Detection pattern:**
Any use of strengths/weaknesses language or high/low as value judgments.

**Correction:**
Replace with cost-frame:
- "Strengths" → "What flows easily" or "What you supply without strain"
- "Weaknesses" → "What carries cost" or "What takes more to sustain"
- "Low [Nature]" → "[Nature] engagement carries cost for you"
- "High [Nature]" → "You supply [Nature] engagement easily"

---

### 1.10 Explanatory Causation

**NEVER generate:**
- "The reason you [behavior] is because of your [Nature]"
- "Your [Nature] makes you..."
- "Because you're [Nature], you..."
- "This explains why you..."
- "[Nature] causes..."

**Detection pattern:**
Any claim that Nature is the cause of specific behaviors.

**Correction:**
Replace with correlation frame:
- "A pattern that may be present..."
- "[Behavior] may be more costly when [Nature] engagement doesn't flow easily"
- Remove causal claim entirely

## Section 2: Contextual Prohibitions

These patterns are problematic in certain contexts.

### 2.1 "Your Nature" (singular)

**Problematic because:**
Implies a person has one defining Nature.

**AVOID:**
- "Your Nature is..."
- "Your primary Nature"
- "Your dominant Nature"

**ACCEPTABLE alternatives:**
- "Natures that show up most easily for you"
- "Engagement patterns that flow with less cost"

---

### 2.2 "Find" Language

**Problematic when implying discovery of hidden truth:**
- "Find your true self"
- "Find your purpose"
- "Find who you really are"

**ACCEPTABLE:**
- "Find language for what you recognize"
- "Find yourself in this description"
- "Find patterns you hadn't named"

---

### 2.3 Test/Assessment Results Framing

**Problematic:**
- "Your results show you ARE..."
- "The test determined that you..."
- "According to your profile, you should..."

**ACCEPTABLE:**
- "Your results suggest..."
- "The assessment surfaces patterns that..."
- "Your profile indicates tendencies toward..."

---

## Section 3: Required Inclusions

Certain content requires explicit statements.

### 3.1 Any Page About the MNTEST Must Include:

- What the test does NOT do (diagnose, predict, prescribe, define identity)
- That it's a starting point, not conclusion
- That results don't tell you what to do

### 3.2 Any Page About the Natures Must Include:

- Statement that Natures describe situational demands, not personal traits
- Statement that everyone can supply all nine
- Statement that the question is cost, not identity

### 3.3 Any Page About Certification Must Include:

- That certification is not license to diagnose or prescribe
- That certified practitioners work within limits
- That the training includes learning where MN stops

### 3.4 Any Educational Content Must Include:

- At least one statement of what MN doesn't do
- Return of agency to the reader
- Avoidance of prescriptive conclusion

## Section 4: Structural Patterns to Avoid

### 4.1 List Formats

**AVOID:**
- "5 Ways to Use Your [Nature]"
- "10 Careers for [Nature] People"
- "Best Jobs for Each Nature"
- "How to Leverage Your Intelligences"

**ACCEPTABLE:**
- "Understanding [Nature] Engagement"
- "What [Context] Demands and What It Costs"

### 4.2 Quiz/Personality Content Patterns

**AVOID:**
- "Which Nature Are You?"
- "Discover Your Type"
- "What Your Nature Says About You"
- "Your Nature Personality"

**ACCEPTABLE:**
- "Explore Your Engagement Patterns"
- "What Shows Up Repeatedly for You"

### 4.3 Comparison Structures

**AVOID:**
- "[Nature] vs. [Nature]"
- "Are you more [Nature] or [Nature]?"
- "The difference between [Nature] people and [Nature] people"

**ACCEPTABLE:**
- "The difference between [Nature] engagement and [Nature] engagement"
- "When situations pull for [Nature] vs. [Nature]"

---

## Section 5: Tone Markers

### 5.1 Avoid Enthusiasm Escalation

**RED FLAGS:**
- Multiple exclamation points
- "Amazing," "incredible," "life-changing"
- Superlatives about MN
- "Revolutionary," "breakthrough"

**PREFERRED:**
- Measured confidence
- Earned claims only
- Let the framework speak for itself

### 5.2 Avoid False Intimacy

**RED FLAGS:**
- "I know you feel..."
- "Like you, I..."
- "We all know that..."
- Presumed shared experience

**PREFERRED:**
- "Some people find..."
- "You may recognize..."
- "What shows up for some is..."

### 5.3 Avoid Defensive Overclaiming

**RED FLAGS:**
- "MN is better than other frameworks"
- "Unlike personality tests, MN..."
- "The problem with other systems is..."

**PREFERRED:**
- Describe what MN does without comparison
- Let readers make their own comparisons

## Section 6: Pre-Publication Checklist

Before publishing ANY content, verify:

### Identity/Typing Check
□ No sentence uses a Nature as a noun for a person
□ No "You are [Nature]" patterns
□ No "[Nature]s are..." patterns

### Prescription Check
□ No "You should..." patterns
□ No career recommendations
□ No relationship prescriptions
□ Agency returned to reader

### Promise Check
□ No transformation claims
□ No prediction claims
□ No completeness claims
□ No urgency language

### Terminology Check
□ No Gardner's original terms
□ "Gross Bodily" or "Fine Bodily" (not "bodily-kinesthetic")
□ "Graphic Visual" or "Spatial Visual" (not "visual-spatial")
□ "Logical" (not "logical-mathematical")

### Scope Check
□ Limits of MN stated or implied
□ No overclaiming what MN can do
□ Frame stays observational

### Tone Check
□ Measured, not enthusiastic
□ Permission-granting, not pressure
□ Warm but contained

## Section 7: Correction Protocols

When drift is detected:

### Level 1: Word Substitution
If only terminology is wrong:
- Substitute correct terms
- Verify sentence still makes sense
- Check surrounding context for drift

### Level 2: Sentence Reframe
If sentence structure creates drift:
- Identify the problematic pattern
- Rewrite to situational frame
- Verify meaning preserved

### Level 3: Section Rewrite
If multiple sentences drift:
- Identify the root cause (usually typing or prescription)
- Rewrite the section maintaining MN frame
- Add explicit limit statement

### Level 4: Full Revision
If the piece fundamentally drifts:
- Start from purpose: what is this content meant to do?
- Rebuild using canon documents as guide
- Review against all checklists before republishing

---

## Section 8: When in Doubt

If uncertain whether something violates canon:

**Ask these questions:**
1. Does this create a category of people?
2. Does this tell someone what to do?
3. Does this promise an outcome?
4. Would someone reading this think MN tells them who they are?
5. Would Steven Rudolph publish this?

**If yes to 1-4 or no to 5:**
Revise before publishing.

**Default behavior:**
When uncertain, err toward:
- Less claim rather than more
- Situational language rather than personal
- Returning agency rather than prescribing
- Stating limits rather than omitting them

The goal is not to be timid. The goal is to be accurate.

Accuracy requires restraint.

Restraint is the competence.
