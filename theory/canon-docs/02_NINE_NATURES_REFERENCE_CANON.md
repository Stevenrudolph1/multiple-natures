# Nine Natures Reference Canon

**For AI-Assisted Content Generation**

---

## Critical Framing Rule

**The Nine Natures are not traits. They are not identities. They describe what situations pull for—not who people are.**

Every description must maintain this frame. The moment a Nature becomes "something someone is" rather than "something a situation asks for and someone supplies," drift has occurred.

---

## The Nine Natures

### 1. PROTECTIVE

**What it describes:**
The engagement that appears when a situation pulls for vigilance, accuracy, boundary-holding, or prevention of error.

**What situations demand Protective engagement:**
- Quality control
- Safety oversight
- Risk assessment
- Error prevention
- Boundary maintenance
- Accuracy verification
- Compliance monitoring

**When supplied easily:**
The person scans for what could go wrong without being asked. They notice gaps, risks, inconsistencies. They hold standards. They feel steady doing this work—it doesn't deplete them to maintain vigilance.

**When it costs:**
In situations that pull away from Protective engagement—environments requiring speed over accuracy, tolerance for ambiguity, or suspension of judgment—the person may tighten, hesitate, or feel anxious. The work gets done but recovery takes longer.

**CORRECT ways to write about Protective:**
- "The role pulls for sustained vigilance—noticing risks before they become problems."
- "She supplies Protective engagement easily in this context."
- "The situation demanded accuracy and boundary-holding."
- "Protective engagement appeared naturally when the task required error prevention."

**INCORRECT ways to write about Protective (NEVER USE):**
- "She is a Protective type."
- "His Protective nature makes him cautious."
- "Protectives are good at..."
- "If you're Protective, you should..."
- "Her strength is being Protective."
- "He has a Protective personality."

## Key Grammatical Patterns

### ALWAYS use these structures:

**For situations:**
- "The situation/role/task **pulls for** [Nature] engagement."
- "The work **demanded** [Nature]."
- "The context **required** [Nature] engagement."

**For people:**
- "She **supplies** [Nature] engagement easily."
- "He **meets** [Nature] demands without strain."
- "[Nature] engagement **appears** naturally for her in this context."
- "The [Nature] **pull** is something she responds to easily."

**For cost:**
- "Supplying [Nature] engagement here **carries cost** for him."
- "The [Nature] demand is **expensive** for her to sustain."
- "She **can supply** [Nature], but it **takes more** to maintain."

### NEVER use these structures:

- "She **is** [Nature]."
- "He **is a** [Nature] type."
- "[Nature]**s** are/do/have..."
- "**Your** [Nature]..."
- "**If you're** [Nature]..."
- "[Nature] **people**..."
- "**As a** [Nature]..."
- "**His/Her** [Nature] **nature**..."

---

## Combination Patterns

When writing about how Natures combine, maintain the situational frame:

**CORRECT:**
"The role pulled for both Administrative and Protective engagement—organization plus vigilance. She supplied both easily, which is why the work felt sustainable."

**INCORRECT:**
"She's Administrative-Protective, which makes her perfect for compliance roles."

**CORRECT:**
"He meets Entrepreneurial demands easily but finds sustained Administrative engagement costly. The startup phase energized him; the operational phase depleted him."

**INCORRECT:**
"He's Entrepreneurial, not Administrative, so he shouldn't be in operations."

---

## The Critical Test

Before publishing any content about the Natures, ask:

1. **Is the Nature attached to a situation or to a person's identity?**
   - Situation = correct
   - Identity = drift

2. **Could this sentence work with "engagement" added after the Nature name?**
   - "Protective engagement" = correct framing
   - "Protective people" = drift

3. **Does this imply what someone should do based on their Nature?**
   - If yes = drift (prescriptive)
   - If no = possibly correct

4. **Does this create a category of people ("Creatives," "Protectives")?**
   - If yes = drift (typing)
   - If no = possibly correct

---

## Summary

The Nine Natures describe modes of engagement that situations demand and people supply. They are not traits, types, or identities. Content must always maintain this frame by:

1. Attaching Natures to situations, not people
2. Using "engagement" language
3. Describing supply and cost, not identity
4. Avoiding prescriptive conclusions
5. Never creating categories of people

When in doubt, reframe the sentence to make the situation the subject and the Nature the demand.
