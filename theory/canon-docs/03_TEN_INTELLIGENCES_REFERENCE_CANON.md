# Ten Intelligences Reference Canon

**For AI-Assisted Content Generation**

---

## Critical Context

Multiple Natures uses a **modified version** of Howard Gardner's Multiple Intelligences framework. This modification is essential and non-negotiable.

**Key Differences from Standard Gardner:**

1. **Gardner's "Bodily-Kinesthetic Intelligence" is split into two:**
   - Gross Bodily Intelligence (full body control, large movements)
   - Fine Bodily Intelligence (hand/fine motor skill, precision work)

2. **Gardner's "Spatial Intelligence" is split into two:**
   - Graphic Visual Intelligence (creating and interpreting 2D images)
   - Spatial Visual Intelligence (3D navigation, spatial reasoning)

3. **This gives us 10 Intelligences, not Gardner's 8**

AI systems are heavily trained on Gardner's original terminology. **You must override this default.** Never use "bodily-kinesthetic" or undifferentiated "spatial."

---

## Critical Framing Rule

**Intelligences describe capacity—the channels through which learning, work, and expression move.**

**Intelligences do NOT describe:**
- What sustains someone
- What energizes vs. drains
- Where someone should work
- Who someone is

**The MN distinction:**
- **Intelligences** = How someone CAN work (channel, capacity)
- **Natures** = What it COSTS to keep working that way (source, energy)

A person can be high in an intelligence and still find sustained use of that intelligence draining. Capacity ≠ sustainability.

## The Ten Intelligences

### 1. GROSS BODILY INTELLIGENCE

**What it describes:**
The capacity to use the whole body for expression, learning, and problem-solving. Large muscle coordination, full-body movement, physical presence.

**NOT the same as:**
- Fine motor skill (that's Fine Bodily)
- Athletic ability (that's a performance outcome, not a channel)
- "Bodily-kinesthetic" (Gardner's undifferentiated term—never use)

**How it shows up:**
- Learning through whole-body movement
- Expressing ideas physically
- Coordinating large muscle groups
- Physical presence and spatial occupation
- Sport, dance, physical labor, performance

**CORRECT usage:**
- "The task required Gross Bodily Intelligence—whole-body coordination."
- "He processes information through movement and physical engagement."
- "She has strong capacity in Gross Bodily Intelligence."

**INCORRECT usage (NEVER USE):**
- "He's bodily-kinesthetic." (Gardner term)
- "She's a physical learner." (identity)
- "Gross Bodily people are..." (typing)
- "If you're high Gross Bodily, you should..." (prescriptive)

---

### 2. FINE BODILY INTELLIGENCE

**What it describes:**
The capacity to use hands and fine motor control for precision work, detailed manipulation, and skilled craft.

**NOT the same as:**
- Whole-body coordination (that's Gross Bodily)
- Artistic talent (that's a performance outcome)
- "Bodily-kinesthetic" (Gardner's undifferentiated term—never use)

**How it shows up:**
- Precision hand work
- Detailed manipulation
- Craft and skilled trades
- Surgery, dentistry, fine arts
- Instrument playing (physical aspect)
- Writing, drawing, assembling

**CORRECT usage:**
- "The work required Fine Bodily Intelligence—precision and dexterity."
- "She processes through her hands, working with fine detail."
- "Strong capacity in Fine Bodily Intelligence."

**INCORRECT usage (NEVER USE):**
- "He's bodily-kinesthetic." (Gardner term)
- "She's good with her hands." (vague/colloquial)
- "Fine Bodily people are..." (typing)
- "Your Fine Bodily strength means..." (prescriptive)

- Perceiving others' emotional states
- Responding to group dynamics
- Noticing what isn't being said

**INCORRECT usage (NEVER USE):**

## The MI-MN Distinction

This is the most critical concept for preventing drift.

**MI tells you HOW someone can work.**
**MN tells you what it COSTS to keep working that way.**

### Example 1:
A person has strong Linguistic Intelligence. They write fluidly and express clearly.

- **MI alone would suggest:** They should write more, take writing roles, develop their "strength."
- **MN adds:** They may or may not be sustained by writing. If writing carries high cost for them despite capacity, continued writing will extract rather than feed.

### Example 2:
A person has strong Interpersonal Intelligence. They read rooms accurately and respond to social cues.

- **MI alone would suggest:** They should work with people, take social roles.
- **MN adds:** They may find sustained social engagement draining despite capacity. The skill doesn't indicate the energy source.

**CORRECT framing:**
"She has strong Interpersonal Intelligence—she reads people accurately. Whether sustained social engagement feeds or depletes her depends on her Natures, not her intelligence."

**INCORRECT framing:**
"She's high Interpersonal, so she should work with people."

## Terminological Mandates

### ALWAYS say:
- Gross Bodily Intelligence
- Fine Bodily Intelligence
- Graphic Visual Intelligence
- Spatial Visual Intelligence
- Logical Intelligence

### NEVER say:
- Bodily-kinesthetic (Gardner's term)
- Visual-spatial (Gardner's undifferentiated term)
- Spatial (alone, undifferentiated)
- Logical-mathematical (Gardner's term)

### ALWAYS use:
- "Capacity in [Intelligence]"
- "The channel of [Intelligence]"
- "Processing through [Intelligence]"
- "Strong/developed [Intelligence]"

### NEVER use:
- "[Intelligence] type"
- "[Intelligence] person"
- "[Intelligence]s are..."
- "As a [Intelligence]..."
- "If you're [Intelligence]..."

## The Critical Test

Before publishing any content about Intelligences, ask:

1. **Am I using the modified MN terms, not Gardner's originals?**
   - "Gross Bodily" not "bodily-kinesthetic"
   - "Graphic Visual" not "visual-spatial"

2. **Am I describing capacity (what they can do) without implying sustainability (what feeds them)?**
   - Capacity = MI territory
   - Sustainability = MN territory

3. **Am I avoiding prescriptive conclusions?**
   - "You should" based on MI alone = drift

4. **Am I keeping intelligence as a channel, not an identity?**
   - "Processes through" = correct
   - "Is a [type]" = drift

## Summary

The Ten Intelligences describe channels of capacity—how people can process, learn, and express. They do NOT describe what sustains people or what they should do.

MN modifies Gardner's framework by splitting Bodily and Visual intelligences to better match practical observation. Always use the MN terms.

The relationship between MI and MN is:
- MI = Channel (how)
- MN = Source (what it costs)

Content must maintain this distinction and never collapse capacity into identity or prescription.
