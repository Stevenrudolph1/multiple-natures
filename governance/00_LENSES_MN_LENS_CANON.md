# MN_LENS_CANON.md
**Canonical Document — Binding on All MN Work Involving Observational Frameworks**

---

## PURPOSE

This canon governs the **use of lenses**, not their content, validity, or outcomes.

A lens is a way of noticing patterns. This canon exists because lenses drift toward authority unless actively prevented.

The danger is not that lenses are false.  
The danger is that lenses stop being tools and start deciding for people.

This document applies to all lenses — including Multiple Natures.

---

## SUPREME CONSTRAINT

**The right to set down any lens is non-negotiable.**

A lens that cannot be set down is not an observational tool.  
It is a claim on the person.

That claim is not permitted.

---

## THE FUNDAMENTAL DISTINCTION

A lens that says **"this is true"** is categorically different from a lens that says **"this helps us notice."**

This Canon allows the second.  
It refuses the first.

That is the line.

---

## THE THREE CLASSES OF LENSES

Not all lenses carry the same risk. The Canon recognizes three classes, each with different constraints.

---

### Class 1: Observational Lenses (Low Risk)

These attempt to notice patterns.

**Examples:**
- Multiple Natures
- Multiple Intelligences
- MBTI
- Enneagram
- OCEAN / Big Five

**Canon rules:**
- Permitted
- Non-authoritative
- Must stop before identity
- Must stop before instruction

These are the safest lenses. They may observe. They may not decide.

---

### Class 2: Interpretive Lenses (Medium Risk)

These shape meaning, not just observation.

**Examples:**
- Narrative identity
- Life stories
- Mythic arcs
- Cultural frames

**Canon rules:**
- Allowed only as reflective tools
- Never allowed to explain the person
- Never allowed to prescribe action
- Never allowed to close meaning

**Permitted use:**  
"Here is one way you might be telling the story."

**Forbidden use:**  
"This is your story."

Interpretive lenses are powerful — which is why they are dangerous. A narrative that helps someone reflect can become a destiny that forecloses their future. The constraint is strict: interpretive lenses may open possibilities, never close them.

---

### Class 3: Totalizing Lenses (High Risk — Not Permitted)

These claim truth, authority, or moral rightness.

**Examples:**
- Religion
- Political ideology
- Moral systems
- Identity-based belief systems

**Canon rules:**
- Not permitted as lenses
- May appear only as contextual facts
- May never be used to interpret, explain, or guide a person

**Why?**  
Because they override personhood by definition.

This does not attack these systems. It simply refuses to use them as lenses. They belong to the person, not to the practitioner. They may shape context, but they never explain the person.

No worldview is allowed to outrank the human.

---

## WHY THIS DISTINCTION MATTERS

The Canon is not anti-lens.  
It is anti-totalizing lens.

A lens is permitted only if it can observe without claiming authority over the person.

That single rule decides everything.

---

## NO LENS IS PRIVILEGED

Among permitted lenses, no lens is ontologically privileged. No lens is canonically exclusive. Different lenses notice different patterns. The Canon does not arbitrate which patterns are more real, more useful, or more true.

All observational and interpretive lenses are subject to the same constraints.

---

## WHY MN APPEARS MORE OFTEN

Multiple Natures appears frequently in this body of work because it is the lens most developed by the author.

This reflects authorial focus, not canonical privilege.

MN is not more true, more comprehensive, or more authoritative than other lenses. Other practitioners may work primarily with other lenses. This is permitted.

What is not permitted is any lens exceeding its observational scope.

---

## WHAT LENSES MAY DO

Lenses may:

- Notice patterns
- Name what is observed (without claiming)
- Offer vocabulary for what someone already senses
- Support attention without directing it
- Be set down at any time

---

## WHAT LENSES MAY NOT DO

Lenses may not:

- Assign identity ("you are a ___ type")
- Predict outcomes ("you will thrive in...")
- Prescribe action ("given your nature, you should...")
- Replace judgment ("the framework says...")
- Override the person's own sense of themselves
- Claim truth over the person

---

## THE LINE (OPERATIONAL)

**Observation:** "This pattern seems present."

**Authority:** "This pattern means you should..."

The first is permitted. The second is not.

When a lens crosses from observation to authority, it stops being a tool and becomes a replacement for the person's own judgment.

That replacement is the violation.

---

## TOTALIZING LENSES ARE FORBIDDEN

Some lenses claim totality:

- "This explains everything about you"
- "All human behavior reduces to these categories"
- "Once you understand this framework, you understand people"

These are not observational lenses. They are authority claims.

Totalizing lenses are not permitted within this Canon — not because they are false, but because they cannot stop. A lens that explains everything cannot be set down.

---

## PROCEDURAL SAFEGUARD

Any use of a lens that results in labeling, dismissing, or explaining a person without their consent is a violation of this constraint.

When this occurs, the correct response is not correction but withdrawal.

The lens user does not argue, justify, or refine. They stop.

---

## WHY THIS MATTERS

Lenses are efficient. They travel faster than context. They outlive the moment that produced them.

This efficiency is also their danger.

A lens that helps one person notice something can become a system that sorts thousands. A vocabulary that supports reflection can become a label that forecloses it.

The constraint exists because lenses drift toward authority unless actively prevented.

---

## READER EXPERIENCE

A person who encounters this Canon should finish feeling *more* permission, not less.

**Not:** "I must be careful with frameworks now."  
**But:** "I no longer have to carry frameworks as truth."

**Not:** "Lenses are dangerous."  
**But:** "I can use lenses without being owned by them."

**Not:** "I should be suspicious of explanations."  
**But:** "Explanations don't get to decide who I am."

If this Canon makes frameworks feel more dangerous rather than more honest, it has failed.

## RELATION TO OTHER CANON

This document serves:

**SEEING_PEOPLE_BEFORE_SYSTEMS**  
No system may replace the person. This canon implements that constraint for all lenses.

**NO_PREDICTION_NO_PRESCRIPTION**  
Observation must stop before instruction. This canon names where that stop occurs for lenses.

**MN_TONE_AND_POSTURE_CANON**  
Limits are structural, not moral. This canon holds lens limits cleanly, without warning or prohibition.

---

## WHAT THIS CANON DOES NOT DO

This canon does not:

- Rank lenses within a class
- Compare lenses competitively
- Recommend specific lenses
- Evaluate lens validity
- Declare any permitted lens superior

Those questions are outside Canon scope.

---

## CANONICAL SUMMARY

The Canon permits observational and interpretive lenses as tools for noticing.

It does not permit lenses that claim truth, authority, or moral rightness over the person.

---

## FINAL CANONICAL STATEMENT

**A lens may help someone notice. A lens may not decide for them.**

The moment a lens is used to explain, categorize, predict, or prescribe — rather than observe — it has exceeded its scope.

This applies to all lenses.

No lens is exempt.
