## Purpose

This document constrains what may be done in the name of Multiple Natures. It governs all MN work including theory, tools, training, and licensing.

It inherits from root governance (`/Projects/governance/GOV-01-orientation.md`) and applies those constraints specifically to MN work.

---

## What This Governs

This governance covers:

- Published MN works (books, frameworks, intellectual property)
- The MNTEST and any other MN assessments
- MN tools, products, and services
- MN training, consulting, and practitioner contexts
- MN licensing and derivative works
- How MN is represented, claimed, and applied

---

## Supreme Constraint (Non-Negotiable)

**No system created from MN may outrank the person it serves.**

Any framework, tool, or assessment built from MN:
- May be set down by the person using it
- May not prescribe, decide for, or override the human
- Stops at observation—never crosses into authority

When any MN tool or application exceeds this boundary, it has violated its function and must be corrected or withdrawn.

This is not a value. It is an operating limit.

(Inherited from root governance GOV-03-constraints-boundaries.md and MN Lens Canon)

---

## What MN May Do

MN work may:

1. **Observe patterns** in how people engage with work, relationship, and responsibility
2. **Name what is observed** without claiming those names are definitive or complete
3. **Offer vocabulary** for what someone already senses but could not articulate
4. **Support orientation** — helping people see where they are without telling them where to go
5. **Reduce confusion** that arises from misreading differences as deficiencies
6. **Be set down** at any time by any person

---

## What MN May Not Do

MN work may not:

1. **Assign identity** — "You are a Creative type" is not permitted
2. **Predict outcomes** — "You will thrive in X role" crosses into authority
3. **Prescribe action** — "Given your natures, you should do X" exceeds observational scope
4. **Replace judgment** — "The framework says..." removes human agency
5. **Override the person's sense of themselves** — MN cannot claim to know someone better than they know themselves
6. **Claim truth over the person** — MN is a lens, not a diagnosis
7. **Continue past orientation** — Once someone is oriented, continuing to apply MN adds pressure, not clarity

**The line:** When MN crosses from observation to authority, it stops being a tool and becomes a claim on the person. That claim is not permitted.

---

## Boundaries Not Traded

The following are not traded for speed, money, approval, scale, or convenience:

1. **MN stops at orientation** — It does not extend into prescription, prediction, or identity
2. **The right to set down MN is non-negotiable** — Any tool, assessment, or framework that cannot be set down has exceeded its function
3. **Context determines whether traits are assets or liabilities** — Never use "strengths and weaknesses" language
4. **Coherence of MN's claims across all outputs** — MN does not contradict itself for market appeal
5. **Human agency and human primacy** — No MN system decides for people

If a partnership, product, opportunity, or license requires violating any of these, the answer is no.

(Inherited from root governance GOV-03-constraints-boundaries.md)

## MN Stops at Orientation

**From MN-GOV-00-foundations.md:**

"MN provided orientation. Once that was achieved, continuing to use it didn't add accuracy. It added pressure. It started asking the framework to decide, to justify, or to move things forward. That wasn't an extension. It was a misuse."

**Operational meaning:**

MN may help someone see patterns. It may clarify where energy goes, what drains them, what sustains them.

**MN may NOT:**
- Tell them what to do next
- Decide which job is right for them
- Prescribe a life direction based on their "configuration"
- Be used continuously as if more MN will create movement

**Why:** What comes after orientation is engagement with consequence—actual choices under real conditions, feedback from reality, movement that involves risk. That work is outside MN's scope.

Recognition does not initiate movement. Engagement does.

MN clarifies. It does not move.

## Licensing and Derivative Works

Ideas from MN are public. They are published in books and available for reference.

**Tools, assessments, and implementations are protected.**

Anyone wishing to create MN-inspired tools, training programs, or derivative works must:
1. Review MN-GOV-20-licensing-framework.md
2. Understand what is permitted, what requires license, and what is prohibited
3. Maintain coherence with MN philosophy and constraints

Revenue comes from applied value, not from restricting ideas.

Licensing exists to prevent distortion, not to prevent use.

(Inherited from root governance GOV-03-constraints-boundaries.md)

## Terminology Requirements

**All MN work must follow MN terminology standards:**

1. **Always capitalize** Multiple Intelligences, Multiple Natures, and all individual Intelligence and Nature names
2. **Never use** "strengths and weaknesses" — always use "assets and liabilities in context"
3. **Use only the 9 listed Natures** — do not invent new ones
4. **Use the modified Gardner framework** — Gross and Fine Bodily Intelligence, not "bodily-kinesthetic"
5. **Never treat MN as identity** — no "you are a ___ type" language
6. **Feed your tigers metaphor** — use consistently when discussing engagement

See: MN terminology skill for complete reference

## IP Posture

**Ideas are open. Implementations are owned. Endorsement is non-transferable.**

- MN theory is public (published in books)
- MN tools (MNTEST, training materials, frameworks) are protected
- License to use MN does not transfer endorsement
- A licensee may use what they acquire however they choose within license terms
- They may not claim the author's approval of how they use it

## Conditions Under Which This May Change

Per root governance (GOV-01):

Governance may change when:
1. Lived experience reveals the current statement to be inaccurate
2. MN work has evolved in ways governance does not yet reflect
3. A principle that seemed stable proves unstable under real conditions

Governance does not change because:
- Someone disagrees with it
- It is inconvenient
- A new idea seems appealing
- It limits business opportunity

**Change requires evidence from use, not argument from preference.**
