## Purpose

This document governs who may use the MNTEST, under what conditions, with what training, and what constitutes misuse.

For technical specifications on what the MNTEST measures and how it works, see MN-GOV-10-mntest-specifications.md.

---

## Supreme Constraint

**The MNTEST may never outrank the person taking it.**

Results:
- Are observations, not conclusions
- Support orientation, do not prescribe action
- May be questioned, rejected, or set down at any time
- Do not determine identity, capability, or worth
- Do not predict outcomes or dictate decisions

When the MNTEST is used in ways that claim authority over the person, it has exceeded its function and must be withdrawn.

(Inherited from MN-GOV-01-orientation.md and root governance GOV-03)

## Who May Administer the MNTEST

### General Principle

The MNTEST may be administered by anyone who:
1. Understands MN theory and its limitations
2. Can hold results as observational, not authoritative
3. Will not use results to prescribe, predict, or assign identity
4. Respects the person's right to reject or set down findings

### Categories of Use

**Personal Use (No Restriction)**
- Anyone may take the MNTEST for their own orientation
- No training or certification required
- Results belong to the individual
- No governance constraint on self-exploration

**Professional Use (Training Required)**
- Using the MNTEST with clients, students, or organizations requires training
- Training ensures understanding of MN boundaries and appropriate use
- Certification (when established) will verify readiness to use responsibly

**Organizational Use (License Required)**
- Organizations wishing to integrate the MNTEST into their practices must obtain a license
- License ensures alignment with MN governance and prevents misuse at scale
- See MN-GOV-20-licensing-framework.md for licensing terms

## Training Requirements

(To be established in MN-GOV-30 series documents)

Until formal training is established, professional use of the MNTEST requires:

1. **Deep familiarity with MN theory**
   - Understanding the 10 Multiple Intelligences and 9 Multiple Natures
   - Knowing the difference between observation and prescription
   - Fluency in assets/liabilities framing (never strengths/weaknesses)

2. **Understanding of MN boundaries**
   - Where MN stops (orientation, not prescription)
   - What MN cannot do (predict, prescribe, assign identity)
   - How to hold results lightly without diminishing their usefulness

3. **Ethical use standards**
   - Respecting the person's primacy over the framework
   - Never using results to justify predetermined conclusions
   - Maintaining confidentiality and appropriate data handling

4. **Contextual awareness**
   - Recognizing that traits are context-dependent (assets vs liabilities)
   - Understanding cultural, life-stage, and situational factors
   - Knowing when the MNTEST is inappropriate for a context

## What Constitutes Misuse

MNTEST misuse includes:

1. **Using results to prescribe action** — "Based on your test, you should..."
2. **Predicting outcomes** — "You will succeed/fail in this role because..."
3. **Assigning identity** — "You are a Creative-Interpersonal type"
4. **Overriding the person's self-knowledge** — "The test knows you better than you know yourself"
5. **Justifying discrimination** — Using results to exclude or limit someone
6. **Continuing past orientation** — Repeatedly applying MN when it's no longer clarifying
7. **Ignoring context** — Treating traits as universal strengths or weaknesses
8. **High-stakes decisions** — Using results for hiring, placement, diagnosis, or legal matters

**Response to misuse:**
- Immediate correction if the interpreter is unaware
- Withdrawal of MNTEST access if misuse continues
- Revocation of license or certification if applicable
