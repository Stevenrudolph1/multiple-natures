## What Multiple Natures Is

Multiple Natures is an **observational framework** for noticing recurring patterns in how people orient themselves toward work, relationship, and responsibility.

It is a **lens**, not a truth claim. It helps notice, not decide.

---

## Core Theory

### The 10 Multiple Intelligences (Modified from Gardner)

MN uses a modified version of Howard Gardner's Multiple Intelligences theory:

1. **Gross Bodily Intelligence** — Full body control and coordination
2. **Fine Bodily Intelligence** — Hand and fine motor skill precision
3. **Interpersonal Intelligence** — Reading and engaging with others
4. **Logical Intelligence** — Pattern recognition and systematic thinking
5. **Linguistic Intelligence** — Facility with language and words
6. **Graphic Visual Intelligence** — Working with images, design, and visual representation
7. **Spatial Visual Intelligence** — Understanding and manipulating space and spatial relationships
8. **Musical Intelligence** — Sensitivity to sound, rhythm, and musical structure
9. **Intrapersonal Intelligence** — Self-awareness and inner reflection
10. **Naturalistic Intelligence** — Recognition of patterns in natural systems

**Key distinctions from Gardner:**
- Gardner's Bodily-Kinesthetic Intelligence is divided into Gross and Fine Bodily
- MN has 10 intelligences; Gardner originally identified 8

**What MIs describe:** How people process information, learn, and engage with their environment.

### The 9 Multiple Natures

MN identifies nine recurring orientations—pulls toward certain kinds of engagement:

1. **Protective Nature** — Preventing harm, loss, injury, mistakes, or wrongdoing
2. **Educative Nature** — Teaching and explaining to others
3. **Administrative Nature** — Organizing, structuring, and getting work done
4. **Creative Nature** — Generating ideas and bringing new forms into existence
5. **Healing Nature** — Tending to others in pain or vulnerability
6. **Entertaining Nature** — Drawing attention, shifting atmosphere, and engaging audiences
7. **Providing Nature** — Supporting and helping others
8. **Entrepreneurial Nature** — Creating or extracting value, initiating, and building
9. **Adventurous Nature** — Taking risks and moving toward novelty or danger

**What MNs describe:** Where energy goes. What someone is drawn toward. What they return to without being asked.

**Critical distinction:** MIs explain **how** someone engages. MNs explain **where** that engagement is directed.

## Swastha and Aswastha

**Swastha** (स्वस्थ)
- Sanskrit: "swa" (self) + "stha" (situated in) = "situated in oneself"
- **Definition:** State of alignment where one's Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures are consistently engaged with activities and work
- Health in MN terms is alignment, not physical metrics

**Aswastha**
- **Definition:** State of misalignment where one's Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures are not engaged with activities
- Leads to compensatory behaviors: overconsumption, distraction, coping mechanisms

**Core principle:** Sustainability begins with swastha. When aligned with purposeful activities that engage their Intelligences and Natures, people derive pleasure from what they do rather than from what they consume. This creates individual sustainability, which leads to sustainable families, communities, and societies.

## Feed Your Tigers

**Core metaphor:** Each Multiple Intelligence and Multiple Nature is a "tiger" that needs to be fed with activities.

**Key concepts:**
- **Your tigers** = your collection of Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures
- **Tiger appetite/size** = level of expression of that MI or MN
- **Feeding** = engaging that MI or MN through activities
- **Overfed tiger** = too much of one MI/MN, leading to burnout
- **Underfed tiger** = neglected MI/MN, creating misalignment

**Final warning:** "Feed your tigers before they eat you!"

## Assets and Liabilities (Never Strengths and Weaknesses)

**Critical principle:** Traits are neutral tools. They are neither inherently good nor bad. Their value depends entirely on context.

**NEVER use:** Strengths and weaknesses language
**ALWAYS use:** Assets and liabilities in context

**Example:**
- Interpersonal Intelligence is an **asset** in a counseling role
- Interpersonal Intelligence is a **liability** as a TSA bag screener (requires ignoring social cues)

**Why this matters:** Like a kitchen knife (good for cutting vegetables, bad for spreading butter), Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures are tools that work well or poorly depending on the situation.

## The 7-Way Test

Seven questions to assess level of each Multiple Intelligence or Multiple Nature:

1. Activities using this trait energize me
2. I feel natural and at ease when engaging in activities using this trait
3. I lose track of time, hunger, or thirst when doing related activities
4. I am self-motivated to pursue related activities
5. I have always been this way, even as a kid
6. I am like this in all contexts—not just at home or work, but everywhere
7. Others, especially those high in this MI or MN, have recognized my talent

## The Menu of Life

**Three categories of activities:**
- **Main Course** — Full-time jobs or long-term commitments
- **Appetizers** — Gigs, part-time jobs, short-term projects
- **Desserts** — Recreational activities, hobbies

Used to describe how different activities feed different tigers in different amounts.

## What MN Does Not Do

Per the MN Lens Canon, Multiple Natures:

**May NOT:**
- Assign identity ("you are a ___ type")
- Predict outcomes ("you will thrive in...")
- Prescribe action ("given your nature, you should...")
- Replace judgment ("the framework says...")
- Override the person's own sense of themselves
- Claim truth over the person

**MAY:**
- Notice patterns
- Name what is observed (without claiming)
- Offer vocabulary for what someone already senses
- Support attention without directing it
- Be set down at any time

**The line:** Observation is permitted. Authority is not.

## MN as Lens, Not System

Multiple Natures is a Class 1 Observational Lens (per MN Lens Canon).

It attempts to notice patterns. It does not attempt to explain the person completely, predict their future, or decide what they should do.

**Supreme constraint:** The right to set down any lens is non-negotiable. A lens that cannot be set down is not an observational tool—it is a claim on the person. That claim is not permitted.

---

## Where MN Stops

From the MN book: "MN provided orientation. Once that was achieved, continuing to use it didn't add accuracy. It added pressure. It started asking the framework to decide, to justify, or to move things forward. That wasn't an extension. It was a misuse."

**MN ends at orientation.**

What comes after orientation is engagement with consequence—movement, not recognition; choice under real conditions, not explanation. That work does not grow out of MN. It exists because MN ends.

MN cannot do that work. It shouldn't try.

## Philosophical Posture

MN is never religious, always practical:
- Draws from all areas of life
- Uses tried and tested principles
- References Eastern wisdom (Sanskrit concepts) and Western science (Gardner's research)
- Never frames concepts religiously or spiritually
- Practical philosophy, not moral or spiritual prescription
