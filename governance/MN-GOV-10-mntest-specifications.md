## Purpose

This document establishes what the MNTEST is, what it measures, how it works, and its validity boundaries.

This is a technical specification document. For governance on who may use the MNTEST and under what conditions, see MN-GOV-11-mntest-governance.md.

---

## What the MNTEST Is

The MNTEST is an **assessment tool** that surfaces patterns in how a person's Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures express themselves.

It is:
- An **orientation tool**, not a diagnostic instrument
- A **pattern highlighter**, not a definitive measure
- A **conversation starter**, not a conclusion
- **Observational**, not prescriptive

It is NOT:
- A personality test
- An identity assignment
- A predictor of success or failure
- A decision-making system
- A complete picture of a person

---

## What the MNTEST Measures

The MNTEST assesses:

### 10 Multiple Intelligences (Modified Gardner Framework)
1. Gross Bodily Intelligence
2. Fine Bodily Intelligence
3. Interpersonal Intelligence
4. Logical Intelligence
5. Linguistic Intelligence
6. Graphic Visual Intelligence
7. Spatial Visual Intelligence
8. Musical Intelligence
9. Intrapersonal Intelligence
10. Naturalistic Intelligence

### 9 Multiple Natures
1. Protective Nature
2. Educative Nature
3. Administrative Nature
4. Creative Nature
5. Healing Nature
6. Entertaining Nature
7. Providing Nature
8. Entrepreneurial Nature
9. Adventurous Nature

### Level of Expression

For each MI and MN, the MNTEST indicates **level of expression**—how strongly that trait tends to show up in a person's life.

**Critical clarification:** Level of expression is not:
- A measure of skill or competence
- A prediction of success
- An indicator of value or worth
- Fixed or unchangeable

Level of expression describes **tendency**, not **destiny**.

### The 7-Way Test Integration

The MNTEST is informed by the 7-Way Test criteria:

1. Activities using this trait energize me
2. I feel natural and at ease when engaging in activities using this trait
3. I lose track of time, hunger, or thirst when doing related activities
4. I am self-motivated to pursue related activities
5. I have always been this way, even as a kid
6. I am like this in all contexts—not just at home or work, but everywhere
7. Others, especially those high in this MI or MN, have recognized my talent

The more of these criteria that are met, the higher the level of expression for that MI or MN.

## Validity Boundaries

### What Results Can Tell You

MNTEST results can:
- **Highlight patterns** that someone already senses but hasn't named
- **Clarify confusion** about why certain situations drain or energize
- **Provide vocabulary** for differences that were being misread
- **Support orientation** — helping someone see where they currently are
- **Reduce friction** in understanding team dynamics or individual preferences

### What Results Cannot Tell You

MNTEST results cannot:
- **Predict performance** in a specific role or context
- **Prescribe career paths** or life decisions
- **Replace observation** of actual behavior over time
- **Explain everything** about a person
- **Determine identity** or categorize someone definitively
- **Guarantee fit** between person and context
- **Tell someone what to do next**

**Critical boundary:** The MNTEST stops at recognition. It does not extend into prediction, prescription, or decision-making.

## Limitations and Disclaimers

### Context Matters

Multiple Intelligences and Multiple Natures are not absolutes. They are patterns that show up with varying strength depending on:
- Life stage and experience
- Current circumstances and pressures
- Cultural context and expectations
- Recent experiences that may temporarily shift expression
- Health, stress, and environmental factors

**Results are a snapshot**, not a permanent categorization.

### Self-Report Limitations

The MNTEST relies on self-report, which means:
- Accuracy depends on self-awareness
- Social desirability bias may influence responses
- People may answer based on how they wish to be, not how they are
- Cultural factors may affect interpretation of questions
- Responses may reflect current state more than long-term pattern

**Best practice:** Results should be validated through observation and conversation, not accepted uncritically.

### Not a Clinical Tool

The MNTEST is not:
- A clinical or diagnostic instrument
- A replacement for professional assessment (psychological, educational, career)
- Validated for high-stakes decisions (hiring, firing, medical, legal)
- Suitable for contexts requiring clinical rigor

**Use case:** Orientation and reflection, not diagnosis or high-stakes decision-making.

## Appropriate Use Cases

The MNTEST is appropriate for:

1. **Personal orientation** — Helping someone clarify their own patterns
2. **Career exploration** — Supporting (not determining) career reflection
3. **Team dynamics** — Understanding why friction may be occurring
4. **Educational context** — Informing (not dictating) pedagogical approach
5. **Coaching and consulting** — Starting conversations about fit and alignment
6. **Training programs** — Introducing MN concepts with lived recognition

**Requirement:** In all cases, results must be held as **observational**, not authoritative.

---

## Inappropriate Use Cases

The MNTEST is NOT appropriate for:

1. **Hiring or firing decisions** — Results do not predict job performance
2. **Educational placement** — Cannot determine academic capability or trajectory
3. **Clinical diagnosis** — Not a psychological or medical tool
4. **Legal or custody decisions** — Not validated for high-stakes contexts
5. **Identity assignment** — Results are not "who someone is"
6. **Replacing observation** — Cannot substitute for watching behavior over time
7. **Justifying predetermined decisions** — Not to be used to rationalize actions already decided

**Violation of appropriate use constitutes misuse of the MNTEST.**

---

## Assets and Liabilities (Context-Dependent)

**Critical principle:** No MI or MN is inherently a strength or weakness. Context determines whether a trait is an asset or liability.

**Example:**
- High Interpersonal Intelligence is an **asset** in a counseling role
- High Interpersonal Intelligence is a **liability** in a role requiring ignoring social cues (e.g., TSA screening)

**Never:**
- Present results as "strengths and weaknesses"
- Suggest certain MIs or MNs are "better" or "more valuable"
- Rank people based on their configuration
- Treat any result as universally positive or negative

**Always:**
- Frame results in terms of fit with specific contexts
- Recognize that all traits have contexts where they are assets and contexts where they are liabilities
- Leave space for the person to interpret their own results

---

## Result Interpretation Requirements

Anyone administering or interpreting MNTEST results must:

1. **Present results as patterns, not identity** — "This suggests..." not "You are..."
2. **Invite the person's interpretation first** — "What do you notice?" before offering observations
3. **Contextualize findings** — Discuss assets and liabilities relative to specific situations
4. **Acknowledge limitations** — Make clear what the test cannot tell us
5. **Support, don't prescribe** — Offer possibilities, never instructions
6. **Allow results to be set down** — The person may reject or ignore findings without penalty

## Validity and Reliability

(To be completed with actual validation data when available)

### Construct Validity
- Does the test measure what it claims to measure?
- How well do results correspond to observable behavior?
- What evidence supports the 10 MIs and 9 MNs framework?

### Test-Retest Reliability
- How stable are results over time?
- What level of variation is expected?
- When should someone retake the assessment?

### Face Validity
- Do results resonate with test-takers?
- How often do people recognize themselves in findings?
- What is the rate of rejection or disagreement with results?

### Ongoing Validation
- How are results tracked and studied over time?
- What feedback mechanisms exist to improve the assessment?
- Who is responsible for validity studies?

## Changes and Updates

The MNTEST may be updated when:
- Evidence from use reveals inaccuracies in measurement
- Better questions surface patterns more clearly
- Validation studies suggest improvements
- Feedback from practitioners and test-takers reveals blind spots

The MNTEST may NOT be changed:
- To increase market appeal
- To align with competing frameworks for commercial reasons
- To make results more flattering or palatable
- To avoid inconvenient findings

**Per root governance (GOV-02):** Change requires evidence from use, not argument from preference.
